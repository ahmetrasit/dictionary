# batch_011 Water Secondary Resonance

## 2:249 — فَلَمَّا فَصَلَ طَالُوتُ بِٱلْجُنُودِ قَالَ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ مُبْتَلِيكُم بِنَهَرٍ فَمَن شَرِبَ مِنْهُ فَلَيْسَ مِنِّى وَمَن لَّمْ يَطْعَمْهُ فَإِنَّهُۥ مِنِّىٓ إِلَّا مَنِ ٱغْتَرَفَ غُرْفَةًۢ بِيَدِهِۦ فَشَرِبُوا۟ مِنْهُ إِلَّا قَلِيلًا مِّنْهُمْ فَلَمَّا جَاوَزَهُۥ هُوَ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ مَعَهُۥ قَالُوا۟ لَا طَاقَةَ لَنَا ٱلْيَوْمَ بِجَالُوتَ وَجُنُودِهِۦ قَالَ ٱلَّذِينَ يَظُنُّونَ أَنَّهُم مُّلَٰقُوا۟ ٱللَّهِ كَم مِّن فِئَةٍ قَلِيلَةٍ غَلَبَتْ فِئَةً كَثِيرَةًۢ بِإِذْنِ ٱللَّهِ وَٱللَّهُ مَعَ ٱلصَّٰبِرِينَ

### شَرِبَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The singular verb denotes actually drinking from the river; that ordinary act is the stated test of allegiance.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A mundane bodily act sorts the army before battle. The surrounding claims about fitness for kingship (2:247), the sign of the ark (2:248), and the victory of a small band (2:250–251) make restraint at water an embodied qualification for belonging and rule.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B002` (an allotted share of water) is retained as a secondary resonance. The verb still means drinking, but the permitted handful makes drinking answerable to a defined portion.

**Activation trace.** The local exception `إِلَّا مَنِ ٱغْتَرَفَ غُرْفَةً بِيَدِهِ` supplies an explicitly bounded quantity, while `مُبْتَلِيكُم بِنَهَرٍ` places it inside a test. That quantity activates B002 as the role of legitimate allotment; the bridge is that the difference between failure and permission is not liquid versus no liquid but unbounded drinking versus the allowed share. The target consequently contributes measured reception, not thirst-satisfaction alone.

**Negative evidence.** The army and the river create broad social and locative imagery, but this singular occurrence does not itself name co-drinkers (B003) or a drinking place (B004). No other branch passes the evidence standard.

### شَرِبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The plural verb reports that the troops drank from the river, with only a few excepted.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The mass act reverses the expected value of abundance: the many drink and falter, while the few who endure become the victorious company. The following prayer for poured-out patience (`أَفْرِغْ عَلَيْنَا صَبْرًا`) further contrasts bodily intake with divinely supplied steadfastness.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers as a group) and `root_000783/B002` (an allotted share of water) are retained. The plural drinking becomes both a social classification and a violation of the visibly bounded allowance.

**Activation trace.** For B003, `بِٱلْجُنُودِ`, `فَشَرِبُوا`, `إِلَّا قَلِيلًا مِّنْهُمْ`, and the later contrast `فِئَةٍ قَلِيلَةٍ`/`فِئَةً كَثِيرَةً` repeatedly sort collective bodies; the branch supplies the role of a drinking-defined company, so the target helps constitute which group can continue with Talut. For B002, the prior `غُرْفَةً بِيَدِهِ` is the permitted share; the bridge is the direct sequence from allowance to the plural report, making their drinking register as excess over an allotment rather than consumption in the abstract.

**Negative evidence.** Nothing describes water quality, absorption, a vessel, or another specialized branch. B003 and B002 exhaust the visible root-local cues.

## 2:259 — أَوْ كَٱلَّذِى مَرَّ عَلَىٰ قَرْيَةٍ وَهِىَ خَاوِيَةٌ عَلَىٰ عُرُوشِهَا قَالَ أَنَّىٰ يُحْىِۦ هَٰذِهِ ٱللَّهُ بَعْدَ مَوْتِهَا فَأَمَاتَهُ ٱللَّهُ مِا۟ئَةَ عَامٍ ثُمَّ بَعَثَهُۥ قَالَ كَمْ لَبِثْتَ قَالَ لَبِثْتُ يَوْمًا أَوْ بَعْضَ يَوْمٍ قَالَ بَل لَّبِثْتَ مِا۟ئَةَ عَامٍ فَٱنظُرْ إِلَىٰ طَعَامِكَ وَشَرَابِكَ لَمْ يَتَسَنَّهْ وَٱنظُرْ إِلَىٰ حِمَارِكَ وَلِنَجْعَلَكَ ءَايَةً لِّلنَّاسِ وَٱنظُرْ إِلَى ٱلْعِظَامِ كَيْفَ نُنشِزُهَا ثُمَّ نَكْسُوهَا لَحْمًا فَلَمَّا تَبَيَّنَ لَهُۥ قَالَ أَعْلَمُ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

### شَرَابِ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** `شَرَابِكَ` is the man's drink or beverage, preserved without the aging expected over a hundred years.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink is not merely provision: alongside food, the ass, and reassembled bones, it is one item in a staged demonstration of death, preservation, and revival. The adjacent challenge about giving life (2:258) and Abraham's request to see how the dead are revived (2:260) frame the beverage as evidence within a lesson about divine power.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B011` (absorbing as understanding) is retained. It supplements the physical beverage with a pedagogic role: what is drink is also something the observer must mentally take in.

**Activation trace.** The repeated command `فَٱنظُرْ ... وَٱنظُرْ ... وَٱنظُرْ`, followed by `فَلَمَّا تَبَيَّنَ لَهُ` and `أَعْلَمُ`, supplies an unusually dense cognition sequence around the target. B011 supplies the role of grasping or absorbing; the abductive bridge is that the preserved drink is explicitly presented to sight until understanding results. The target therefore contributes an evidentiary intake while remaining an ordinary beverage.

**Negative evidence.** The later mention of wealth and seed in 2:261 is too remote and indirect to activate the wealth-consumption or watered-growth branches. No container, allotment, or water quality is specified.

## 6:70 — وَذَرِ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱتَّخَذُوا۟ دِينَهُمْ لَعِبًا وَلَهْوًا وَغَرَّتْهُمُ ٱلْحَيَوٰةُ ٱلدُّنْيَا وَذَكِّرْ بِهِۦٓ أَن تُبْسَلَ نَفْسٌۢ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ لَيْسَ لَهَا مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ وَلِىٌّ وَلَا شَفِيعٌ وَإِن تَعْدِلْ كُلَّ عَدْلٍ لَّا يُؤْخَذْ مِنْهَآ أُو۟لَٰٓئِكَ ٱلَّذِينَ أُبْسِلُوا۟ بِمَا كَسَبُوا۟ لَهُمْ شَرَابٌ مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ وَعَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْفُرُونَ

### شَرَابٌ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** `شَرَابٌ` denotes the drink assigned to the condemned, specified immediately as being from scalding water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Something ordinarily restorative is folded into the consequence of self-earned captivity. The movement from playful immersion in false discourse (6:68, 6:70) to helpless exposure, painful drink, and final gathering (6:72) turns consumption into recompense rather than relief.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `مِنْ حَمِيمٍ` and `عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ` intensify the ordinary drink but do not activate another branch of ش ر ب. The captivity, accounting, and reversal cues alter the ayah-level force of the drink without supplying a root-local allotment, social-drinker, absorption, vessel, or understanding construction.

**Negative evidence.** `بِمَا كَسَبُوا` establishes deserved consequence, not the B010 idiom of charging someone with what was not done. The text gives no visible cue for any other ش ر ب branch.

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** `حَمِيمٍ` qualifies the drink as intensely hot or scalding water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The hot liquid appears precisely where every protective relation has failed. The next ayah depicts companions calling a bewildered person toward guidance, sharpening the contrast between available human voices and the absence of an effective protector at judgment.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000001/B011` (near kin or intimate friend) is retained contrastively. The scalding water remains hot water, while the cognate branch makes it a grim substitute at the point where intimacy and aid are explicitly denied.

**Activation trace.** `لَيْسَ لَهَا ... وَلِىٌّ وَلَا شَفِيعٌ` supplies the absence of protective closeness, and `لَهُ أَصْحَابٌ يَدْعُونَهُ` in 6:71 supplies the nearby companion image. B011 contributes the role of the intimate helper; the abductive bridge is the placement of `حَمِيم` after the denial of every such relation. The target's heat thus carries an added relational deprivation: instead of a حميم who helps, there is حميم to drink.

**Negative evidence.** The binding consequences could broadly suggest fate, but no ح م م form or precise fate/death construction independently activates B010 strongly enough. Blackness, fever, bathhouse, animal, and sound branches lack local triggers.

## 7:31 — يَٰبَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ خُذُوا۟ زِينَتَكُمْ عِندَ كُلِّ مَسْجِدٍ وَكُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟ وَلَا تُسْرِفُوٓا۟ إِنَّهُۥ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُسْرِفِينَ

### ٱشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The imperative permits people to drink, paired with eating, while forbidding excess.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window joins bodily consumption to upright worship, adornment, pure devotion, and true versus imagined guidance. The following ayat reject invented prohibition but immediately distinguish genuinely forbidden conduct, so drinking occupies a disciplined middle between deprivation and excess.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B002` (an allotted share of water) is retained. The permission to drink is heard with the secondary contour of a bounded portion.

**Activation trace.** `وَلَا تُسْرِفُوا` is an immediate quantitative boundary on `وَٱشْرَبُوا`, while the preceding `بِٱلْقِسْطِ` (7:29) supplies a nearby norm of due measure. B002 supplies the role of an appropriate share; the bridge is that non-excess turns open permission into reception within a limit. The target therefore contributes measured enjoyment, not unrestricted intake.

**Negative evidence.** `ٱلطَّيِّبَاتِ مِنَ ٱلرِّزْقِ` supports the goodness of provision but does not specify unpleasant water, a vessel, a drinking group, or another branch. No further resonance qualifies.

## 7:160 — وَقَطَّعْنَٰهُمُ ٱثْنَتَىْ عَشْرَةَ أَسْبَاطًا أُمَمًا وَأَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَىٰ مُوسَىٰٓ إِذِ ٱسْتَسْقَىٰهُ قَوْمُهُۥٓ أَنِ ٱضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ ٱلْحَجَرَ فَٱنۢبَجَسَتْ مِنْهُ ٱثْنَتَا عَشْرَةَ عَيْنًا قَدْ عَلِمَ كُلُّ أُنَاسٍ مَّشْرَبَهُمْ وَظَلَّلْنَا عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْغَمَٰمَ وَأَنزَلْنَا عَلَيْهِمُ ٱلْمَنَّ وَٱلسَّلْوَىٰ كُلُوا۟ مِن طَيِّبَٰتِ مَا رَزَقْنَٰكُمْ وَمَا ظَلَمُونَا وَلَٰكِن كَانُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ يَظْلِمُونَ

### ٱسْتَسْقَىٰ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The people ask Moses to obtain water for them.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Their request is answered not by a single served drink but by a durable, differentiated system: twelve peoples, twelve springs, and a known drinking-place for each. The preceding guidance and equitable dealing (7:158–159) make the water arrangement a material image of ordered guidance and justice.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B002` (providing a watering source) is retained. The request for water opens into access to continuing sources rather than a one-time handed drink.

**Activation trace.** `ٱضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ ٱلْحَجَرَ`, `ٱثْنَتَا عَشْرَةَ عَيْنًا`, and `قَدْ عَلِمَ كُلُّ أُنَاسٍ مَّشْرَبَهُمْ` visibly describe generated sources assigned for use. B002 supplies the role of durable water provision; the bridge is the narrative expansion from asking for water to receiving springs from which each group can drink. The target thus contributes infrastructure and access as well as a request for immediate relief.

**Negative evidence.** No prayer formula is quoted, so B006 is not retained merely because divine provision follows. The springs are for people rather than agricultural land, leaving B003 without its irrigation-specific trigger.

### ٱنۢبَجَسَتْ — ب ج س

**Primary reading.** The verb depicts the twelve springs breaking or gushing out from the struck stone.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A single hard stone answers a collective need through twelve distinct outlets. The correspondence between twelve tribes and twelve springs turns emergence into an ordered distribution rather than an undifferentiated flood.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The stone, the striking, `مِنْهُ`, the twelve `عَيْنًا`, and the following drinking-places strongly instantiate liquid breaking out through an opening, which is the verb's primary branch. None of those cues shifts the exact root toward its only other inventoried branch.

**Negative evidence.** No person is called `مُنْبَجِس` or otherwise described as abundant in good, so `root_000084/B002` is not activated. The abundant benefit of the springs is thematic resemblance, not the required person-quality construction.

### مَّشْرَبَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** `مَّشْرَبَهُمْ` is each people's known drinking-place or assigned access to water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Knowing where to drink is embedded in a numerical correspondence between twelve tribal communities and twelve springs. The water-site is therefore also an instrument for preventing confusion and maintaining communal order.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers as a group) is retained. The drinking-place remains a place/share, but it also identifies a water-organized social body.

**Activation trace.** `ٱثْنَتَىْ عَشْرَةَ أَسْبَاطًا أُمَمًا`, the matched `ٱثْنَتَا عَشْرَةَ عَيْنًا`, and especially `كُلُّ أُنَاسٍ` visibly attach each source to a human collective. B003 supplies the role of a company constituted around common watering; the bridge is the one-to-one mapping of group and `مَشْرَب`. The target consequently marks social membership and coordination, not location alone.

**Negative evidence.** The branch of allotted water is already close to the noun's primary contribution here; it is not counted again as a secondary reading. No quality, absorption, vessel, or body-part cue appears.

## 10:4 — إِلَيْهِ مَرْجِعُكُمْ جَمِيعًا وَعْدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقًّا إِنَّهُۥ يَبْدَؤُا۟ ٱلْخَلْقَ ثُمَّ يُعِيدُهُۥ لِيَجْزِىَ ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ وَعَمِلُوا۟ ٱلصَّٰلِحَٰتِ بِٱلْقِسْطِ وَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَهُمْ شَرَابٌ مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ وَعَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌۢ بِمَا كَانُوا۟ يَكْفُرُونَ

### شَرَابٌ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** `شَرَابٌ` is the condemned people's drink, immediately specified as scalding water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink is placed inside the cosmic sequence of origin, return, and just recompense. The surrounding ordering of creation, sun, moon, years, and reckoning (10:3–6) makes even this act of consumption part of an exact moral order.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `لِيَجْزِىَ`, `بِٱلْقِسْطِ`, and `بِمَا كَانُوا يَكْفُرُونَ` make the drink consequential and deserved, but they do not supply a second ش ر ب image. Its punitive force remains an ayah-level reframing of ordinary drink.

**Negative evidence.** There is no bounded share, drinking company, absorption, vessel, or cognition sequence tied to this target. No secondary branch passes the standard.

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** `حَمِيمٍ` denotes the intensely hot water from which the punitive drink consists.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Heat is presented not as random violence but as part of a promised return and measured recompense. The surrounding regularity of created order reinforces that precision.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000001/B010` (being decreed, fated, or death close) is retained. It adds the contour of fixed adjudication to the hot water without replacing its thermal sense.

**Activation trace.** `إِلَيْهِ مَرْجِعُكُمْ جَمِيعًا`, `وَعْدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقًّا`, `يُعِيدُهُ`, and `لِيَجْزِىَ` form an explicit sequence of certain return and decreed consequence. B010 supplies the role of unavoidable fate; the bridge is that the target appears as the concrete content of that true promise. The hot water is thus read as an enacted verdict, not merely a painful substance.

**Negative evidence.** The qualified mention of intercession in 10:3 concerns divine governance and does not visibly establish intimate kinship, so B011 is not retained. Blackness, fever, bathhouse, sound, and animal/property branches have no triggers.

## 16:66 — وَإِنَّ لَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَنْعَٰمِ لَعِبْرَةً نُّسْقِيكُم مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهِۦ مِنۢ بَيْنِ فَرْثٍ وَدَمٍ لَّبَنًا خَالِصًا سَآئِغًا لِّلشَّٰرِبِينَ

### نُّسْقِي — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb presents God as giving people pure milk to drink from livestock.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink emerges clean and easy to swallow from between waste and blood. Set after rain revives dead earth (16:65) and before fruits become drink and good provision (16:67), the act belongs to a sequence of life-giving transformations from hidden or mixed sources.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B005` (fluid gathered in the belly) is retained. The act of giving drink acquires a secondary emphasis on extracting drinkable fluid from bodily enclosure.

**Activation trace.** `مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهِ` is an exact bodily-container cue, and `مِنۢ بَيْنِ فَرْثٍ وَدَمٍ` identifies the difficult internal environment from which `لَبَنًا خَالِصًا` emerges. B005 supplies the role of enclosed belly-fluid; the bridge is that the liquid being given is explicitly sourced inside the animal's belly. The target consequently foregrounds transformation and release from enclosure, while still meaning that drink is given.

**Negative evidence.** The text does not describe agricultural irrigation, prayer for rain, a dyeing process, or hostility forced into a heart. Those س ق ي branches do not qualify.

### شَّٰرِبِينَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The plural participle denotes the people who drink the milk, with `سَائِغًا` marking it as easy to pass or swallow.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drinkers receive an unmingled, palatable result from a bodily setting named by waste and blood. Their ordinary act is therefore the endpoint of a concealed purification.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained contrastively. It does not make the milk unpleasant; it sharpens the ayah's explicit insistence that this drink is the opposite.

**Activation trace.** `لَبَنًا خَالِصًا سَائِغًا لِّلشَّارِبِينَ` places purity and effortless palatability directly against `فَرْثٍ وَدَمٍ`. B005 supplies the contrastive role of liquid that can be drunk despite disagreeable quality; the bridge is the ayah's marked removal of precisely such resistance. The target thus denotes drinkers experiencing unhindered pleasantness, not merely agents of intake.

**Negative evidence.** The plural form by itself is the ordinary designation of drinkers and is insufficient to retain the specialized co-drinker/social-watering branch B003. No allotment, vessel, absorption, or cognition cue appears.

## 16:69 — ثُمَّ كُلِى مِن كُلِّ ٱلثَّمَرَٰتِ فَٱسْلُكِى سُبُلَ رَبِّكِ ذُلُلًا يَخْرُجُ مِنۢ بُطُونِهَا شَرَابٌ مُّخْتَلِفٌ أَلْوَٰنُهُۥ فِيهِ شِفَآءٌ لِّلنَّاسِ إِنَّ فِى ذَٰلِكَ لَءَايَةً لِّقَوْمٍ يَتَفَكَّرُونَ

### شَرَابٌ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** `شَرَابٌ` is a drinkable liquid issuing from the bees, described as varied in color and containing healing for people.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window repeatedly converts what is taken in into a new benefit: fruit is eaten, paths are followed, and a healing drink exits the belly. The next ayah's movement from knowledge to loss of knowledge places this transformation beside another contrast between hidden capacity and visible outcome.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B007` (permeating or being imbued, including color suffusion) is retained. The drink remains a liquid, but its marked colors and healing capacity make it a carrier of qualities that enter and affect.

**Activation trace.** `مُّخْتَلِفٌ أَلْوَانُهُ` is a direct color-suffusion cue, while `فِيهِ شِفَاءٌ لِّلنَّاسِ` assigns an inward effect to what the liquid carries. B007 supplies the role of a medium imbued with color and capable of permeating a recipient; the bridge is the explicit movement from varied quality within the drink to healing in people. The target therefore contributes transmission of qualities as well as potable substance.

**Negative evidence.** `يَتَفَكَّرُونَ` states the audience's reflection but does not turn drinking itself into understanding, so B011 is not retained. Nearby provision does not instantiate wealth being eaten and drunk, and no allotment or vessel is specified.

## 19:26 — فَكُلِى وَٱشْرَبِى وَقَرِّى عَيْنًا فَإِمَّا تَرَيِنَّ مِنَ ٱلْبَشَرِ أَحَدًا فَقُولِىٓ إِنِّى نَذَرْتُ لِلرَّحْمَٰنِ صَوْمًا فَلَنْ أُكَلِّمَ ٱلْيَوْمَ إِنسِيًّا

### ٱشْرَبِ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The imperative tells Mary to drink, paired with eating, as immediate bodily provision and comfort.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Eating and drinking are joined to settling the eye and then to abstaining from speech. The window moves from a stream below and ripe dates above (19:24–25) to her carrying the child before an accusing community (19:27–28), so private nourishment precedes public ordeal.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B004` (drinking place or source) and `root_000783/B014` (watered land or green growth) are retained. The command remains literal drinking while gathering the prepared location and its fruit-bearing vegetation into the act.

**Activation trace.** For B004, `قَدْ جَعَلَ رَبُّكِ تَحْتَكِ سَرِيًّا` gives an immediately local source beneath her; the branch supplies the role of a divinely arranged drinking-place, so `ٱشْرَبِى` becomes reception from a situated provision rather than an unspecified liquid. For B014, `جِذْعِ ٱلنَّخْلَةِ` and `رُطَبًا جَنِيًّا` place fresh growth beside that source; the branch supplies watered vegetation, and the bridge is the paired command `فَكُلِى وَٱشْرَبِى`, which unites fruit and water. The target consequently links nourishment from stream and plant.

**Negative evidence.** No measured portion, unpleasant water, social drinking company, absorption, or understanding construction appears. The comforting phrase `قَرِّى عَيْنًا` is not enough by itself to activate the heart-affection portion of B007.

## 23:33 — وَقَالَ ٱلْمَلَأُ مِن قَوْمِهِ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ وَكَذَّبُوا۟ بِلِقَآءِ ٱلْءَاخِرَةِ وَأَتْرَفْنَٰهُمْ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنْيَا مَا هَٰذَآ إِلَّا بَشَرٌ مِّثْلُكُمْ يَأْكُلُ مِمَّا تَأْكُلُونَ مِنْهُ وَيَشْرَبُ مِمَّا تَشْرَبُونَ

### يَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The verb says that the messenger drinks from the same kinds of provision as his audience.

**Ayah-level surprise.** An ordinary sign of shared humanity is made into the elite's argument against obedience and resurrection. The preceding call to worship (23:32) and following warning that obedience would be loss (23:34–35) expose how common consumption is rhetorically converted into grounds for disbelief.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers) is retained. His drinking is made to locate him inside the audience's consuming company.

**Activation trace.** `بَشَرٌ مِّثْلُكُمْ`, the parallel `يَأْكُلُ مِمَّا تَأْكُلُونَ`, and the matched `يَشْرَبُ مِمَّا تَشْرَبُونَ` insist on shared action and source. B003 supplies the role of co-drinker; the bridge is the speakers' use of common food and drink to establish social sameness. The target therefore contributes fellowship in provision, even though the speakers weaponize that fellowship against his message.

**Negative evidence.** `مِمَّا` names shared source or kind, not an assigned water-share. No vessel, quality, absorption, or cognition cue supports another branch.

### تَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The second-person plural verb denotes what the addressed people themselves drink and supplies the comparison for the messenger's drinking.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The audience's own ordinary consumption becomes the standard by which they try to disqualify a messenger. Within the window, shared embodiment is treated as incompatible with guidance even though the messenger is explicitly sent `مِّنْهُمْ` (23:32).

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers) is retained. The audience's drinking defines the social company into which the messenger is rhetorically placed.

**Activation trace.** `مِّنْهُمْ`, `مِثْلُكُمْ`, and the exact reciprocal parallel between his eating/drinking and `تَأْكُلُونَ`/`تَشْرَبُونَ` create a visible co-participation construction. B003 supplies the group of co-drinkers; the bridge is that “what you drink” functions as a boundary-marker of common human life. The target consequently contributes collective identity, not only the audience's act of consuming liquid.

**Negative evidence.** The plural audience alone would be insufficient, but the explicit reciprocal comparison supplies B003; no equivalent trigger exists for B002 or the remaining branches.
