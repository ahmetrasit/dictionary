# batch_006 Water Secondary Resonance

## 56:68 — أَفَرَءَيْتُمُ ٱلْمَآءَ ٱلَّذِى تَشْرَبُونَ

### مَآءَ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The word denotes the actual water regularly consumed by the addressees.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding language of loss and deprivation (لَمُغْرَمُونَ، مَحْرُومُونَ) turns an ordinary drink into a conspicuous mercy, while the following question relocates its source in the clouds and contrasts gratitude with the possibility of bitter salinity.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B003` is retained: the water is heard not only as a substance but as something actively supplied.

**Activation trace.** The explicit source-question ءَأَنتُمْ أَنزَلْتُمُوهُ مِنَ ٱلْمُزْنِ أَمْ نَحْنُ ٱلْمُنزِلُونَ supplies the local action of bringing water down. Because B003 covers supplying or putting water at another's disposal, the descent bridges ordinary water to bestowed provision; مَآءَ therefore carries giver-dependent availability in addition to its material sense.

**Negative evidence.** No cue supports plating, sheen, crystal, heart-dullness, or reproductive fluid. Mere cloud origin does not by itself establish the abundance conditions of B002.

### تَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The verb denotes the addressees' ordinary act of drinking the water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Drinking is framed as a contingent privilege: the same water could be made أُجَاجًا, so the familiar act implicitly depends on the drink's present quality.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` is retained: the threatened أُجَاجًا activates the branch of unpleasant, saline, or bitter water that may be drunk only with aversion or need.

**Activation trace.** The sequence تَشْرَبُونَ ... جَعَلْنَٰهُ أُجَاجًا places actual drinking against a counterfactual deterioration in taste. B005 supplies the water-quality role; the abductive bridge is that the act remains lexically “drinking” while its ease and desirability can be withdrawn. The target thus contributes grateful, quality-dependent drinking rather than mere ingestion.

**Negative evidence.** Nothing locally marks an allotted turn, a group or place of drinkers, bodily channels, absorption, neck-binding, craning, understanding, or the minor technical and idiomatic branches.

## 67:30 — قُلْ أَرَءَيْتُمْ إِنْ أَصْبَحَ مَآؤُكُمْ غَوْرًا فَمَن يَأْتِيكُم بِمَآءٍ مَّعِينٍۭ

### مَآؤُ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The first occurrence denotes the addressees' water supply considered as “your water,” hypothetically sunk beyond reach.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The surrounding alternatives of destruction or mercy and the declaration of reliance make material access to water another test of whom one can actually depend upon.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` is retained through a sharply visible reversal: the branch of water appearing or entering the land is activated by its threatened disappearance into غَوْرًا and by the contrasted مَآءٍ مَّعِينٍ.

**Activation trace.** أَصْبَحَ مَآؤُكُمْ غَوْرًا pictures water going deep, while the second half asks who can bring accessible water. B002 supplies the appearance/access role. The bridge is contrastive: what is possessed as “your water” is not a fixed object but water whose emergence can be reversed. The possessive target consequently becomes precarious and contingent.

**Negative evidence.** The first occurrence is not itself an act of watering or pouring, so B003 is not retained here. No local cue supports B004-B008.

### مَآءٍ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The second occurrence denotes accessible, flowing or evident water offered as the answer to the hypothetical loss.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The interrogative فَمَن converts a basic resource into evidence about agency: after water withdraws, its return cannot be assumed.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` and `root_001458/B003` are retained. B002 makes the water an event of renewed appearance after غَوْرًا; B003 makes it a provision that someone must bring.

**Activation trace.** The contrast غَوْرًا ... مَعِينٍ visibly opposes hidden depth to accessible water, activating B002. The construction فَمَن يَأْتِيكُم بِمَآءٍ explicitly asks for an agent who brings it, activating B003. Together, the branches change the target from an unnamed quantity of water into water re-emerging and being supplied to dependent recipients.

**Negative evidence.** No visible feature activates reproductive fluid, coating, sheen, crystal, or heart-dullness.

## 69:11 — إِنَّا لَمَّا طَغَا ٱلْمَآءُ حَمَلْنَٰكُمْ فِى ٱلْجَارِيَةِ

### مَآءُ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The word denotes floodwater that rose beyond its ordinary bounds.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The same overwhelming water that belongs to the sequence of punitive seizure becomes the medium around a saving act, حَمَلْنَٰكُمْ فِى ٱلْجَارِيَةِ, and then a remembered lesson.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` is retained: طَغَا is an exact local cue for water becoming excessive or abundant.

**Activation trace.** The predicate طَغَا assigns the water surplus, transgressive rise. B002 supplies the role of water appearing or becoming abundant; the bridge is direct rather than thematic. The target therefore contributes not neutral water but water whose increased quantity has become an agent-like historical threshold between destruction and carriage.

**Negative evidence.** Being carried in the vessel does not say that water entered it, and no intentional watering or pouring is stated; B003 is therefore not retained. B004-B008 lack local triggers.

## 72:16 — وَأَلَّوِ ٱسْتَقَٰمُوا۟ عَلَى ٱلطَّرِيقَةِ لَأَسْقَيْنَٰهُم مَّآءً غَدَقًا

### أَسْقَيْ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb promises that abundant water would be provided to them if they remained straight on the path.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The following لِّنَفْتِنَهُمْ فِيهِ makes plentiful provision itself a test, not an uncomplicated sign of approval.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The object مَّآءً غَدَقًا confirms the ordinary provision-of-water reading. It does not visibly introduce an irrigation share or channel, bodily fluid, prayer formula, rain cloud, reed, dyeing, or forced heart-imbuing.

**Negative evidence.** B003 is tempting because abundance could irrigate land, but no land, crop, share, or channel appears. The spiritual context alone is insufficient for B010.

### مَّآءً — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the abundant water promised as provision.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its abundance is expressly made an instrument of testing by لِّنَفْتِنَهُمْ فِيهِ, so material plenty bears moral pressure.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` and `root_001458/B003` are retained. B002 intensifies the noun as water in abundance; B003 frames that abundance as intentionally supplied.

**Activation trace.** غَدَقًا visibly supplies B002's abundance role. The causative لَأَسْقَيْنَٰهُم supplies B003's watering/provision role. The bridge joins quantity and agency: this is not merely water but an ample allotment deliberately made available, which sharpens how it can function as a test.

**Negative evidence.** No trigger supports reproductive fluid, plating, sheen, crystal, or heart-dullness.

## 77:20 — أَلَمْ نَخْلُقكُّم مِّن مَّآءٍ مَّهِينٍ

### مَّآءٍ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The word denotes the lowly reproductive fluid from which human beings are created.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The next two ayat reverse the apparent insignificance of مَّهِينٍ: the fluid is placed in a secure repository until a known measure, joining humble material to ordered protection and timing.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B003` is retained alongside the primary reproductive-fluid sense: the sequence activates the branch of putting water into something.

**Activation trace.** The pronoun in فَجَعَلْنَٰهُ فِى قَرَارٍ مَّكِينٍ returns directly to the target and gives it a visible placement construction. B003 supplies the role of water intentionally put into a receptacle; the bridge is the immediate anaphoric sequence. The target thus contributes material that is not merely emitted but deliberately emplaced and held.

**Negative evidence.** B002's abundance or seepage is absent, and the secure repository does not activate coating, sheen, crystal, or heart-dullness.

## 77:27 — وَجَعَلْنَا فِيهَا رَوَٰسِىَ شَٰمِخَٰتٍ وَأَسْقَيْنَٰكُم مَّآءً فُرَاتًا

### أَسْقَيْ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb states that the addressees were provided fresh water to drink.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The provision appears within a compact inventory of the earth's holding, the living and dead, and lofty stabilizing mountains, then is immediately set against denial.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** مَّآءً فُرَاتًا fixes the act as ordinary provision of drinkable water. The window shows neither an agricultural water-share nor a channel, bodily fluid, prayer, rain cloud, reed, dyeing, or coerced inward absorption.

**Negative evidence.** Earth and mountains supply setting, not evidence for irrigation. No additional س ق ي branch passes the standard.

### مَّآءً — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the fresh, sweet water provided to the addressees.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Freshness is not isolated sensory description; it belongs to the earth's ordered capacity to hold life and death and sustain its inhabitants.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B003` is retained: the target is explicitly the object of أَسْقَيْنَٰكُم and therefore resonates as supplied water.

**Activation trace.** The construction وَأَسْقَيْنَٰكُم مَّآءً names both supplier, recipients, and supplied substance. B003 contributes the intentional-watering role; the bridge is direct syntactic government. The noun consequently carries the force of provision, not only freshwater substance.

**Negative evidence.** No abundance term activates B002, and no local trigger supports B004-B008.

### فُرَاتًا — ف ر ت

**Primary reading.** The adjective characterizes the water as fresh and sweet.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its palatability completes the provision: the ayah does not merely mention water's presence but specifies its fitness for human use before rebuking denial.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The immediate head noun مَّآءً securely realizes the ordinary fresh-water branch. Nothing in the window points to a particular river or to a weakening intellect.

**Negative evidence.** Neither `root_001137/B002` nor `root_001137/B003` has a visible local trigger; retaining either would replace evidence with homonymy.

## 78:14 — وَأَنزَلْنَا مِنَ ٱلْمُعْصِرَٰتِ مَآءً ثَجَّاجًا

### مَآءً — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes rainwater sent down from the rain-bearing clouds.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The following purpose sequence, لِّنُخْرِجَ بِهِۦ حَبًّا وَنَبَاتًا وَجَنَّٰتٍ, extends a downward torrent into the emergence of food, growth, and dense gardens.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` is retained: descent from ٱلْمُعْصِرَٰتِ and the modifier ثَجَّاجًا visibly activate abundant outpouring.

**Activation trace.** أَنزَلْنَا ... مَآءً ثَجَّاجًا presents both origin and heavy flow. B002 supplies the role of water appearing in abundance from the sky; the bridge is exact to the branch inventory. The target therefore carries eventive, fecund abundance rather than denoting rainwater only as matter.

**Negative evidence.** The crop result does not itself say that a person or object was watered, so B003 is not separately retained. B004-B008 have no cues.

### ثَجَّاجًا — ث ج ج

**Primary reading.** The adjective depicts the rainwater as pouring forth copiously.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its force is immediately interpreted by what that torrent produces: grain, vegetation, and interwoven gardens rather than destructive flooding.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** مَآءً and أَنزَلْنَا directly anchor the ordinary pouring/outflow sense. Although vegetation follows, there is no meadow defined by descending basins, and no speech setting activates the eloquent-speaker branch.

**Negative evidence.** `root_000195/B002` is single-source and lacks its required basin/meadow image; `root_000195/B003` has no speaking cue. Neither passes the higher burden.

## 79:31 — أَخْرَجَ مِنْهَا مَآءَهَا وَمَرْعَىٰهَا

### مَآءَ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The word denotes the earth's water, brought forth from it together with its pasture.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The paired possessives مَآءَهَا وَمَرْعَىٰهَا bind hidden resource to visible sustenance, while the settled mountains and final مَتَٰعًا لَّكُمْ وَلِأَنْعَٰمِكُمْ place both within a prepared habitat.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B002` is retained: أَخْرَجَ مِنْهَا is an exact cue for water appearing from the earth.

**Activation trace.** The verb-preposition sequence makes the earth the source from which its water emerges. B002 contributes the appearance/seepage role; the bridge is the overt خروج construction. The target thus becomes latent water made manifest and usable, not a static possession of the earth.

**Negative evidence.** Pasture and livestock show benefit but not an explicit act of watering, so B003 is not retained. No cue supports B004-B008.

## 80:25 — أَنَّا صَبَبْنَا ٱلْمَآءَ صَبًّا

### مَآءَ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the water poured down as the first stated stage in producing human food.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The command to inspect food unfolds it backward into coordinated acts: emphatic pouring, splitting the earth, and causing grain to grow. The familiar food thus contains an unseen water-to-earth process.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001458/B003` is retained: the doubled صَبَبْنَا ... صَبًّا explicitly activates the branch of supplying water by pouring.

**Activation trace.** Both verb and cognate verbal noun visibly assign forceful, intentional pouring to the target. B003 supplies the role of water being delivered into the productive environment; the bridge is direct lexical construction. The target therefore contributes administered provision at the head of the food sequence, not merely liquid substance.

**Negative evidence.** Growth is a result, but no cue specifically activates the fruit-sheen branch B006. There is also no independent appearance, reproductive, coating, crystal, or heart-water trigger.

## 86:6 — خُلِقَ مِن مَّآءٍ دَافِقٍ

### مَّآءٍ — م و ه

**Primary reading.** The word denotes the reproductive fluid described as forcefully emitted and as the material from which the human is created.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window moves from every soul being guarded, through examination of its lowly bodily origin, to divine capacity for its return. Origin and return frame the transient fluid within preservation and re-creation.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** دَافِقٍ and يَخْرُجُ clarify the motion of the reproductive fluid and reinforce its ordinary branch; they do not supply the well, land-seepage, sky-rain, or abundance conditions of B002, nor an intentional watering or pouring act for B003.

**Negative evidence.** The reproductive sense already accounts for the local image. رَجْعِهِۦ belongs to a different root and cannot by itself activate another م و ه branch; B005-B008 likewise lack local triggers.
