# batch_008 Water Secondary Resonance

## 17:66 — رَّبُّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى يُزْجِى لَكُمُ ٱلْفُلْكَ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ لِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِۦٓ إِنَّهُۥ كَانَ بِكُمْ رَحِيمًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the broad body of water through which God drives ships so that people may seek His bounty; its ordinary contribution is the navigable setting of commerce and provision.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The same sequence moves from protected servants and divine trusteeship (17:65), through profitable navigation, to distress at sea and danger even after reaching land (17:67–68). The ship's routine motion is therefore framed as mercy amid a vulnerability that neither sea nor land removes.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained. The sea contributes not only a location but the undertaking of sea travel by which bounty is sought under divine care.

**Activation trace.** The visible construction `يُزْجِى لَكُمُ ٱلْفُلْكَ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ لِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِ` names ship movement, a maritime route, and its commercial purpose. These cues activate `root_000086/B009` in the role of practiced sea-going. The abductive bridge is that a sea explicitly traversed by ships for livelihood naturally evokes the root's branch for embarking upon and being related to the sea. This changes `بَحْرِ` from passive scenery into a mercifully enabled field of travel and work.

**Negative evidence.** The following distress sharpens the ayah-level risk but does not itself establish bewilderment in this focus ayah, so `root_000086/B008` is not retained here. No visible cue establishes cleaving, saltiness, exposed-place idiom, disease, thirst, or the remaining branches.

## 17:67 — وَإِذَا مَسَّكُمُ ٱلضُّرُّ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ ضَلَّ مَن تَدْعُونَ إِلَّآ إِيَّاهُ فَلَمَّا نَجَّىٰكُمْ إِلَى ٱلْبَرِّ أَعْرَضْتُمْ وَكَانَ ٱلْإِنسَٰنُ كَفُورًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the place where severe harm reaches travelers, false objects of appeal disappear, and God alone is called upon before rescue to land.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding ayah presents the sea as a route to bounty and mercy, while the following ayat deny that land is finally secure and threaten a return to sea and drowning. Rescue changes location but not human dependence; the striking reversal is the rescued person's immediate turning away.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) and `root_000086/B008` (bewilderment and fear-struck daze) are retained. The first makes the crisis a hazard internal to an ongoing maritime undertaking; the second adds the disorienting collapse of ordinary reliance under sea danger.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000086/B009`, `ٱلْفُلْكَ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ` in 17:66 and `يُعِيدَكُمْ فِيهِ تَارَةً أُخْرَىٰ` in 17:69 visibly place the focus between embarkation and possible re-embarkation. The branch's role is sea-going activity, and the bridge is the travel sequence surrounding the distress; `بَحْرِ` becomes a traveled medium whose benefits and dangers are inseparable. For `root_000086/B008`, the cues are `ٱلضُّرُّ`, the disappearance expressed by `ضَلَّ مَن تَدْعُونَ`, and the threatened `قَاصِفًا` and `فَيُغْرِقَكُم`. The branch's role is experiential disorientation: extreme peril strips away the traveler's normal map of helpers. Thus the sea contributes a crisis of orientation and allegiance, not merely physical danger.

**Negative evidence.** The land/sea contrast does not activate saltiness, cleaving, water-holding ground, or open-exposure idiom. The particularly remote or single-source disease, medical, belly, encounter, and thirst branches have no local trigger.

## 17:70 — وَلَقَدْ كَرَّمْنَا بَنِىٓ ءَادَمَ وَحَمَلْنَٰهُمْ فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ وَرَزَقْنَٰهُم مِّنَ ٱلطَّيِّبَٰتِ وَفَضَّلْنَٰهُمْ عَلَىٰ كَثِيرٍ مِّمَّنْ خَلَقْنَا تَفْضِيلًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea, paired with land, is one of the domains across which God carries humankind as part of their honor, provision, and preference.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Immediately before this statement, the sea can engulf and the land can collapse or be struck; immediately after it, people are summoned, judged, and may be blind to the way. Human dignity is therefore not invulnerability but the gift of being carried through perilous created domains and held answerable for that gift.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained. It makes the sea member of the land/sea pair an enacted domain of human transport, not merely a geographic category.

**Activation trace.** The direct cue `وَحَمَلْنَٰهُمْ فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ` assigns carriage across the sea, while the preceding `يُعِيدَكُمْ فِيهِ` and `فَيُغْرِقَكُم` keep actual sea-going visible. `root_000086/B009` functions as the travel branch. The bridge is that being carried in the sea is the concrete condition from which the root's sea-travel usage arises. Consequently `بَحْرِ` contributes a bestowed human capacity and relation to the maritime realm, folded into `كَرَّمْنَا`.

**Negative evidence.** `كَثِيرٍ` and repeated preference express comparison and quantity, not the lexical breadth in knowledge, wealth, pasture, speech, or running delimited by `root_000086/B002`; that branch is not retained. No other branch has a visible local trigger.

## 18:60 — وَإِذْ قَالَ مُوسَىٰ لِفَتَىٰهُ لَآ أَبْرَحُ حَتَّىٰٓ أَبْلُغَ مَجْمَعَ ٱلْبَحْرَيْنِ أَوْ أَمْضِىَ حُقُبًا

### بَحْرَيْنِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The two seas identify the meeting place Moses resolves to reach, even if he must continue for a long span.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A geographic destination follows ayat about fixed appointments for judgment and destruction (18:58–59), then becomes a place recognizable through a forgotten fish's path rather than by direct human mastery (18:61–62). Determined travel reaches its marker through an event the travelers initially overlook.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) and `root_000086/B003` (wide cleft) are retained. The two seas become both the horizon of a sustained journey and a meeting whose water is marked by a passage-like opening.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000086/B009`, `أَبْلُغَ`, `أَمْضِىَ حُقُبًا`, `جَاوَزَا`, and `سَفَرِنَا` form a visible travel sequence directed toward the two seas. The branch's role is maritime destination and relation; the bridge is that persistent journeying to the sea-junction makes `بَحْرَيْنِ` an organizing term of travel, not a static label. For `root_000086/B003`, the adjacent construction `فَٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ سَرَبًا` supplies a path and tunnel-like passage within the water. The branch's role is opening/corridor, and the bridge is that a `سَبِيل` described as `سَرَبًا` visibly presents the sea as admitting a cleft-like route. This adds to the meeting of seas the image of water containing an opened sign-path.

**Negative evidence.** The long duration `حُقُبًا` is temporal persistence, not enough by itself to activate `root_000086/B002` breadth. The journey is deliberate, so the single-source unplanned-encounter branch `root_000086/B010` is not retained; the other branches lack local triggers.

## 18:61 — فَلَمَّا بَلَغَا مَجْمَعَ بَيْنِهِمَا نَسِيَا حُوتَهُمَا فَٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ سَرَبًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the water into which the forgotten fish takes its path, described as a tunnel or passage.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The travelers reach the intended meeting yet fail to recognize the decisive sign there; only after passing it, feeling fatigue, and recalling the fish does the sea-path become `عَجَبًا`. Arrival and recognition are separated by forgetfulness.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B003` (wide cleft) and `root_000086/B008` (bewilderment and stupefaction) are retained. The first makes the water body appear opened into a corridor; the second makes that corridor the object around which forgetfulness turns into astonished recognition.

**Activation trace.** `سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ سَرَبًا`, repeated in 18:63 as `سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ عَجَبًا`, visibly activates `root_000086/B003`: `سَبِيل` plus `سَرَبًا` gives the branch the role of a cleft-like channel. The bridge is the concrete image of a route maintaining itself inside water, so `بَحْرِ` contributes a medium opened into a sign-bearing passage. The same repetition activates `root_000086/B008` through `نَسِيَا`, `أَنسَىٰنِيهُ`, and especially `عَجَبًا`. Its role is cognitive disorientation followed by amazement; the bridge is that the strange sea-path is both missed and then marveled at. The target therefore also marks a disturbance in perception and memory.

**Negative evidence.** `سَفَرِنَا` is a general human journey, while the explicit sea-path belongs to the fish; this is insufficient to add the more specific sea-embarkation branch `root_000086/B009`. No cue supports saltiness, exposed-place idiom, ground, disease, encounter, thirst, or the remaining branches.

## 18:63 — قَالَ أَرَءَيْتَ إِذْ أَوَيْنَآ إِلَى ٱلصَّخْرَةِ فَإِنِّى نَسِيتُ ٱلْحُوتَ وَمَآ أَنسَىٰنِيهُ إِلَّا ٱلشَّيْطَٰنُ أَنْ أَذْكُرَهُۥ وَٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ عَجَبًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the medium in which the fish takes its extraordinary path, now recalled as an astonishing event.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Forgetting the fish seems like failure, yet remembering its path reverses the travelers onto their traces and leads them to a servant endowed with mercy and special knowledge (18:64–65). The missed sea-sign becomes the guide to what they were seeking.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B003` (wide cleft), `root_000086/B008` (bewilderment and stupefaction), and `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth, including in knowledge) are retained. Respectively, the sea is opened as a strange route, experienced through disoriented wonder, and positioned as the threshold to an enlargement of knowledge.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000086/B003`, `ٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُۥ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ`, together with the earlier `سَرَبًا`, is the visible image of a corridor through water. The branch serves as the opening/channel image; the bridge is that a tunnel-like sea-path resembles a cleft maintained in the water, changing `بَحْرِ` into an opened guideway. For `root_000086/B008`, `نَسِيتُ`, `أَنسَىٰنِيهُ`, and `عَجَبًا` explicitly supply disrupted recollection and amazement. The branch serves as the observer's cognitive state, and the sea consequently contributes the startling sign that overturns failed orientation. For `root_000086/B002`, the immediate result of retracing the sea-sign is `وَعَلَّمْنَٰهُ مِن لَّدُنَّا عِلْمًا`; knowledge is an expressly delimited scope of this branch. Its role is epistemic breadth. The bridge is the tight sequence from the remarkable sea-path, to reversed tracks, to encounter with divinely given knowledge; the sea becomes a threshold at which the travelers' field of knowledge is expanded.

**Negative evidence.** Although `فَوَجَدَا عَبْدًا` is a meeting, the window does not say it was an accidental encounter of the special kind delimited by the single-source `root_000086/B010`; it is not retained. No visible trigger supports the remaining branches.

## 18:79 — أَمَّا ٱلسَّفِينَةُ فَكَانَتْ لِمَسَٰكِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ فَأَرَدتُّ أَنْ أَعِيبَهَا وَكَانَ وَرَآءَهُم مَّلِكٌ يَأْخُذُ كُلَّ سَفِينَةٍ غَصْبًا

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the workplace of poor boat owners whose vessel is deliberately damaged so that a king will not seize it.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding demand for payment and declaration of parting give way to an explanation in which an apparent defect protects vulnerable workers. The following explanations maintain the same reversal from visible harm to a concealed merciful purpose.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained. Here it contributes an occupational maritime relation: the sea is not background but the workers' sphere of livelihood and exposure to royal seizure.

**Activation trace.** `ٱلسَّفِينَةُ`, `لِمَسَٰكِينَ يَعْمَلُونَ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ`, and the repeated `كُلَّ سَفِينَةٍ` are direct cues of seafaring work. They activate `root_000086/B009` in the role of sustained livelihood and relation to the sea. The bridge is explicit: those who `يَعْمَلُونَ` in the sea necessarily live through the root's sea-going branch. This makes `بَحْرِ` denote the poor owners' economic world, which the damaged vessel preserves.

**Negative evidence.** `أَعِيبَهَا` states a defect but not a cut or cleft, so it does not meet the standard for `root_000086/B003`. Royal seizure creates vulnerability but supplies no formulaic open-exposure expression for `root_000086/B005`. No other branch has a sufficient local trigger.

## 18:109 — قُل لَّوْ كَانَ ٱلْبَحْرُ مِدَادًا لِّكَلِمَٰتِ رَبِّى لَنَفِدَ ٱلْبَحْرُ قَبْلَ أَن تَنفَدَ كَلِمَٰتُ رَبِّى وَلَوْ جِئْنَا بِمِثْلِهِۦ مَدَدًا

### بَحْرُ (18:109:4) — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The first sea is imagined as an immense reservoir of ink for writing the words of the speaker's Lord.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding permanent gardens are places their inhabitants never seek to exchange, yet even the apparently immense sea can be exchanged for another like it and still prove finite before God's words. The following ayah then places this immeasurable revelation in the speech of a human messenger who declares one God.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth, including breadth in speech) is retained. It intensifies the first sea as the chosen image of maximal material amplitude set in service of unbounded divine words.

**Activation trace.** The visible cues are `مِدَادًا`, `لِّكَلِمَٰتِ رَبِّى`, and the added `بِمِثْلِهِۦ مَدَدًا`. `root_000086/B002` has the role of breadth in verbal expression and supply. The bridge is that the sea's vast physical abundance is converted into writing material precisely to measure `كَلِمَٰت`, while even an equal reinforcement is contemplated. Thus the first `بَحْرُ` contributes not only water volume but the broadest imaginable material capacity for speech, against which the divine words exceed measure.

**Negative evidence.** Ink imagery does not make saltiness, cleaving, sea travel, exposed ground, color/depth, disease, or thirst operative. No other branch is retained.

### بَحْرُ (18:109:9) — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The second sea is the same hypothetical ink reservoir viewed at the point of exhaustion: it would run out before the Lord's words do.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The repeated noun changes function within one sentence, moving from seemingly inexhaustible supply to the exhausted term of comparison. Even duplication by an equal sea cannot close the gap between created quantity and divine words.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth, including breadth in speech) is retained. On this second occurrence, the branch makes maximal breadth itself visibly bounded and exhaustible.

**Activation trace.** `لَنَفِدَ ٱلْبَحْرُ`, contrasted directly with `قَبْلَ أَن تَنفَدَ كَلِمَٰتُ رَبِّى`, and followed by `بِمِثْلِهِۦ مَدَدًا`, activates `root_000086/B002`. Its role is the limit-case of expansive supply and speech. The bridge is the deliberate contest between the sea's proverbial amplitude and the non-exhaustion of `كَلِمَٰت`; the second `بَحْرُ` therefore contributes the collapse of even maximal created breadth before divine expression.

**Negative evidence.** Repetition supplies no trigger for the root's unrelated or higher-burden branches. No secondary branch other than `root_000086/B002` is retained for this occurrence.

## 20:77 — وَلَقَدْ أَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَىٰ مُوسَىٰٓ أَنْ أَسْرِ بِعِبَادِى فَٱضْرِبْ لَهُمْ طَرِيقًا فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ يَبَسًا لَّا تَخَٰفُ دَرَكًا وَلَا تَخْشَىٰ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the water through which Moses is commanded to make a dry path for the servants' night escape, without fear of pursuit.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding garden rivers reward purification, while this sea becomes dry passage for the faithful and then returns as engulfing water over Pharaoh's forces. One water setting sustains permanence; the other differentiates rescue from destruction through the path God commands.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B003` (wide cleft) and `root_000086/B008` (bewilderment and fear-struck daze) are retained. The first makes the sea an explicitly struck-open corridor; the second is activated by fear language only to be negated for Moses while the pursuing force is overwhelmed.

**Activation trace.** `فَٱضْرِبْ لَهُمْ طَرِيقًا فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ يَبَسًا` visibly activates `root_000086/B003`: striking a dry path within water gives the branch the role of a wide opening. The bridge is direct physical correspondence between a cleft and the path produced inside the sea; `بَحْرِ` becomes an opened escape-way without ceasing to denote the water body. For `root_000086/B008`, the explicit contrast `لَّا تَخَٰفُ دَرَكًا وَلَا تَخْشَىٰ`, followed by the pursuers being covered by `ٱلْيَمِّ`, supplies fear and overwhelming danger. The branch's role is a denied psychological effect for the rescued travelers. The bridge is that the sea would ordinarily induce fear-struck disorientation under pursuit, but the command converts that possible experience into confident passage. The target therefore contributes danger whose bewildering force is suspended for one group.

**Negative evidence.** The dry route is not ordinary embarkation or riding upon water, so `root_000086/B009` is not retained. The adjacent rivers and sea do not by shared water vocabulary activate saltiness or water-holding ground. No other branch has a visible cue.

## 22:65 — أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ سَخَّرَ لَكُم مَّا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ وَٱلْفُلْكَ تَجْرِى فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ بِأَمْرِهِۦ وَيُمْسِكُ ٱلسَّمَآءَ أَن تَقَعَ عَلَى ٱلْأَرْضِ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِۦٓ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ بِٱلنَّاسِ لَرَءُوفٌ رَّحِيمٌ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the navigable water in which ships run by God's command as one instance of creation being subjected for people.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Rain greening the earth, ownership of heaven and earth, ship motion, restraint of the sky, and human life, death, and revival are placed in one sequence. Routine navigation is thus one local expression of the same command that sustains the world's larger order, despite human ingratitude.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained. It turns the sea from a named element within creation into the active maritime relation made available to people by divine command.

**Activation trace.** `وَٱلْفُلْكَ تَجْرِى فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ بِأَمْرِهِۦ` directly names ships running in the sea, while `سَخَّرَ لَكُم` states their availability to people. These cues activate `root_000086/B009` in the role of enabled sea-going. The bridge is the exact travel construction: relation to the sea is realized through vessels moving in it. Hence `بَحْرِ` contributes a divinely ordered human capability, not merely the ships' location.

**Negative evidence.** Rain and green earth alter the ayah's larger water-and-life frame but do not activate the root-specific ground branch `root_000086/B006`. The heaven/earth scale is not a lexical cue for `root_000086/B002`, and no other branch passes the evidence standard.
