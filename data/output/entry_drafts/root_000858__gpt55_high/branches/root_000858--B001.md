### Concept and boundary

#### English

This branch treats صِرَاط (ṣirāṭ), سِرَاط (sirāṭ), and زِرَاط (zirāṭ) as a road or path, with the central source image of a straight road. The concept is not merely movement, guidance, or a moral label; it is the named track itself, a الطريق (al-ṭarīq) whose defining shape in the strongest source formulation is الاستقامة (al-istiqāma), straightness. The V4 boundary also preserves the orthographic and phonetic variants سِرَاط (sirāṭ) and زِرَاط (zirāṭ), while keeping the road sense distinct from the swallowing branch and the sword branch.

#### Türkçe

Bu dal, صِرَاط (ṣirâṭ), سِرَاط (sirâṭ) ve زِرَاط (zirâṭ) biçimlerini yol veya özellikle dosdoğru yol kavramı içinde tutar. Buradaki merkez, sadece yönelme ya da hidayet fikri değil, الطريق (eṭ-ṭarîḳ) olarak izlenen hattın kendisidir; en açık kaynak ifadesinde bu hat المستقيم (el-müstaḳîm), yani düz ve sapmasız olarak nitelenir. V4 sınırı, سِرَاط (sirâṭ) ve زِرَاط (zirâṭ) varyantlarını korur, fakat yutma/geçiş dalı ile keskin kılıç dalını bu yol anlamına katmaz.

#### What belongs to the branch

- صِرَاط (ṣirāṭ / ṣirâṭ) as the road/path, especially the straight road.
- سِرَاط (sirāṭ / sirâṭ) as a stated variant for the same road/path sense.
- زِرَاط (zirāṭ / zirâṭ) as a Sihah-attested variant for the road/path sense.

#### What does not belong to the branch

- سَرَطَ الطَّعَام (saraṭa al-ṭaʿām / seraṭa eṭ-ṭaʿâm), swallowing food so it disappears in passage.
- سِرَاط (sirāṭ / sirâṭ) as a cutting sword passing through a blow.

### Source audit

#### Sihah

- Relationship: `explicit_support`
- Reference: `sihah:file=0393IbnHammadJawhari.SihahTajLugha.Shamela0023235-ara1:section=heading%3A2775:headword=%D8%B5%D8%B1%D8%B7:root=%D8%B5%20%D8%B1%20%D8%B7:sha=cb37cabc31ec8035`
- Contribution: Sihah directly states that the three forms name the road/path and supplies a poetic road example.

> الصراط والسراط والزراط: الطريق.

English transliteration: al-ṣirāṭ wa-al-sirāṭ wa-al-zirāṭ: al-ṭarīq.

Türkçe çevriyazı: eṣ-ṣirâṭ ve's-sirâṭ ve'z-zirâṭ: eṭ-ṭarîḳ.

English explanation: The quotation explicitly makes صِرَاط (ṣirāṭ), سِرَاط (sirāṭ), and زِرَاط (zirāṭ) road/path forms.

Türkçe açıklama: Alıntı صِرَاط (ṣirâṭ), سِرَاط (sirâṭ) ve زِرَاط (zirâṭ) biçimlerini açıkça yol anlamına bağlar.

Examples or special analysis: The cited verse line includes وضح الصراط (waḍaḥ al-ṣirāṭ / vaḍaḥ eṣ-ṣirâṭ), the plain/open part of the road.

#### Mufradat

- Relationship: `explicit_support`
- Reference: `mufradat:file=0502RaghibIsbahani.Mufradat.Shamela0023636-ara1:section=heading%3A850:headword=%D8%B5%D8%B1%D8%B7:root=%D8%B5%20%D8%B1%20%D8%B7:sha=a2c60739a5ace108`
- Contribution: Mufradat narrows the road image to the straight road and records سِرَاط (sirāṭ / sirâṭ) as a variant.

> الصراط: الطريق المستقيم. ويقال له: سراط

English transliteration: al-ṣirāṭ: al-ṭarīq al-mustaqīm. wa-yuqālu lahu: sirāṭ.

Türkçe çevriyazı: eṣ-ṣirâṭ: eṭ-ṭarîḳ el-müstaḳîm. ve-yüḳâlü lehu: sirâṭ.

English explanation: The phrase gives the branch's strongest formulation: صِرَاط (ṣirāṭ) is the straight road, with سِرَاط (sirāṭ) as another form.

Türkçe açıklama: İfade dalın en belirgin sınırını verir: صِرَاط (ṣirâṭ), dosdoğru yoldur; سِرَاط (sirâṭ) ise aynı adlandırmanın başka biçimidir.

Examples or special analysis: Mufradat cites Quran 6:153 as an example, but this branch draft does not assign that occurrence to a branch.

#### Maqayis 2074 and 1774

- Relationship: `additional_nuance`
- Reference: `0395IbnFarisQazwini.MucjamMaqayis.JK008008-ara1:heading:2074; 0395IbnFarisQazwini.MucjamMaqayis.JK008008-ara1:heading:1774`
- Contribution: Maqayis treats ص ر ط (ṣ-r-ṭ / ṣ-r-ṭ) as an ibdāl form referred to the sīn entry, then reports an etymological explanation tying سِرَاط (sirāṭ / sirâṭ) to disappearance by passage. This explains overlap with B002 without merging the road branch into swallowing.

> صرط من باب الإبدال وقد ذكر في السين وهو الطريق

English transliteration: ṣaraṭa min bāb al-ibdāl wa-qad dhukira fī al-sīn wa-huwa al-ṭarīq.

Türkçe çevriyazı: ṣeraṭa min bâb el-ibdâl ve-ḳad ẕükire fi's-sîn ve-hüve eṭ-ṭarîḳ.

English explanation: The ص ر ط (ṣ-r-ṭ) entry identifies the form as an exchange/variant and still gives الطريق (al-ṭarīq), the road.

Türkçe açıklama: ص ر ط (ṣ-r-ṭ) maddesi biçimi bir ibdâl/varyant olarak açıklar, ama anlamı yine الطريق (eṭ-ṭarîḳ), yani yol olarak verir.

Examples or special analysis: The Maqayis etymological note is compatible nuance, not a reason to collapse B001 into B002.

### Target-language distinction notes

#### English

English “path” and “way” are usable only when the reader still sees that صِرَاط (ṣirāṭ) is evidenced as الطريق (al-ṭarīq), especially الطريق المستقيم (al-ṭarīq al-mustaqīm). Familiar “Straight Path” is a recognition phrase, not a substitute for the lexicographic boundary. Target-language source verification for the English conventional phrase remains an editorial gap in this test candidate.

#### Türkçe

Türkçe “dosdoğru yol” bu dal için en uygun kısa karşılıktır; yine de “yol” kelimesi سَبِيل (sebîl) gibi başka Arapça kavramları da karşılayabildiği için ayrımı tek başına taşımaz. “Sırat” tanıma terimidir; Türkçe kullanım kaynakları kontrol edilmediği için modern çağrışımı bu taslakta kesinleştirilmemiştir.
