# Water Cohort Profiles: Rain, Quality, and Motion

## Scope and method

This is a neutral pre-analysis baseline for the strict water-word cohorts assigned to this report. It uses only the corresponding root packets and QAC word/ayah data. For each focus ayah, the observation window is the focus ayah plus two preceding and two following ayat within the same surah. The report describes primary usage and repeated contextual properties; it does not propose secondary branch activation or latent resonance.

The strict set in this file contains 51 eligible target morphemes, 43 per-cohort focus-ayah assignments, and 40 distinct focus ayat. The difference is caused by repeated targets in a focus ayah and by the overlap of `فرات` with `أجاج` at 25:53 and 35:12 and of `آسن` with hot-water `حميم` at 47:15.

Exclusions applied mechanically before profiling:

- `حميم`: excluded friend/relative senses at 26:101, 40:18, 41:34, 69:35, and 70:10, as well as `يحموم` at 56:43.
- `سيل/سالت`: excluded molten copper `أَسَلْنَا` at 34:12.
- `وابل`: excluded `وبال` and `وبيل` forms.

Counts below distinguish target morphemes from focus ayat. A singleton cohort has no internally recurring property; its entry records only the properties of its one attested window.

## حميم: hot water

**Eligible set:** 14 target morphemes in 14 focus ayat: 6:70, 10:4, 22:19, 37:67, 38:57, 40:72, 44:46, 44:48, 47:15, 55:44, 56:42, 56:54, 56:93, 78:25.

**Recurring agents and actions.** The hot water is overwhelmingly an administered or encountered punishment rather than an ordinary natural resource. Repeated actions are drinking or being made to drink (6:70, 10:4, 37:67, 47:15, 56:54, 78:25), tasting (38:57), pouring from above (22:19, 44:48), being dragged or circulating in it or around it (40:72, 55:44), and being lodged in an environment containing it (56:42, 56:93). Human control is absent; the recipients are the judged, disbelieving, tyrannical, criminal, or denying groups named in the surrounding windows.

**Recurring spatial schema.** Three placements recur: entry into the body as drink, downward application onto the head and body, and enclosure or movement within a punitive environment. The windows often carry the water from exterior space to bodily interior: 22:19-20 moves from pouring over heads to melting what is in bellies and skins, while 47:15 explicitly moves from being given hot water to the cutting of intestines.

**Recurring outcomes.** `حميم` reverses normal water functions. It does not cool, refresh, or sustain; it intensifies heat, pain, bodily damage, and confinement. It is frequently paired with another punitive substance or condition: painful punishment (6:70, 10:4), fire or hell (22:19-21, 38:55-59, 40:71-74, 44:45-50, 55:43-46, 56:92-95), `غساق` (38:57, 78:25), `سموم` and `يحموم` (56:42-44), or `زقوم` (37:65-68, 56:52-56).

**Recurring rhetorical frames.** The dominant frame is eschatological recompense. Several windows explicitly connect the outcome to prior disbelief or denial (6:70, 10:4, 78:26-27), and several contrast the punished with believers, the God-fearing, or companions of the right (22:17-19, 47:14-17, 55:45-46, 56:91-93). The word therefore appears in moral sorting scenes more often than in standalone physical description.

**Subclusters.** The main subcluster is ingestion or taste (6:70, 10:4, 37:67, 38:57, 47:15, 56:54, 56:93, 78:25). A second subcluster is external exposure, immersion, or circulation (22:19, 40:72, 44:48, 55:44, 56:42). 44:46 is a comparison of boiling rather than a separate act of administering water.

**Outliers.** 47:15 is the sharpest internal contrast: unchanging paradise water and other desirable rivers are set against hot water that cuts the recipients' intestines. 44:46 uses `الحميم` as the comparison standard for boiling. 55:44 uniquely presents repeated circulation between hell and fully heated water. 56:93 calls it a `نزل`, placing it in a reception/provision slot while retaining a punitive outcome.

## أجاج: intensely salty or bitter water

**Eligible set:** 3 target morphemes in 3 focus ayat: 25:53, 35:12, 56:70.

**Recurring agents and actions.** God is the explicit agent who sets water conditions: releasing two bodies of water and placing a barrier (25:53), contrasting two seas while enabling benefits from both (35:12), and stating the ability to make descending drinking water `أجاج` (56:68-70). The repeated human action is drinking or evaluating drinkability; 35:12 calls the contrasting fresh water's drink easy to swallow, and 56:68 begins from water actually drunk.

**Recurring spatial schema.** In 25:53 and 35:12, `أجاج` occupies one side of a two-body contrast with `عذب فرات`. The contrast remains materially bounded at 25:53 by `برزخا` and `حجرا محجورا`. At 56:70, the same quality is applied counterfactually to water descending from clouds, shifting from horizontal bodies to a vertical provision chain.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** The quality marks impaired drinkability without implying total uselessness: 35:12 explicitly retains food, ornament, navigation, and seeking divine bounty from both seas. All three windows place water quality inside an argument for divine power or provision. Gratitude is explicit at 35:12 and 56:70.

**Subclusters.** The paired-water subcluster is 25:53 and 35:12. The transformation counterfactual is 56:70.

**Outlier.** 56:70 is not a presently observed salty body and does not contain `فرات`; it is a hypothetical alteration of potable rainwater used to expose dependence and motivate gratitude.

## آسن: changed or stale water

**Eligible set:** 1 target morpheme in 1 focus ayah: 47:15.

**Window properties.** The form occurs only under negation, `ماء غير آسن`, in a promised garden's rivers. Its immediate series contrasts different desirable liquids by stable qualities: water not changed, milk whose taste has not changed, wine pleasurable to drinkers, and clarified honey. The same focus ayah then contrasts this provision with hot water given to the people of the fire, with bodily destruction as the result. The two ayat before and after frame the contrast as guided people on clear proof versus people following ornamented desire, followed by listeners whose hearts are sealed versus those increased in guidance.

**Spatial schema and outcome.** The water is a flowing river within an abundant garden, and its defining outcome is preservation rather than deterioration. Because the cohort is a singleton, no property can be claimed as recurring across independent occurrences.

**Outlier status.** The single occurrence is itself the whole cohort; there is no within-cohort outlier test.

## بئر: well

**Eligible set:** 1 target morpheme in 1 focus ayah: 22:45.

**Window properties.** The well is not shown supplying water. It is `معطلة`, an abandoned or nonfunctioning element in the remains of a destroyed wrongdoing settlement, paired with collapsed roofs and a raised palace. The surrounding window moves from named peoples who rejected messengers, through divine seizure and ruined infrastructure, to an appeal to travel, reason with hearts, and hear, followed by warning about the promised punishment.

**Spatial schema and outcome.** A normally vertical access point to a shared resource is presented as unused amid architectural ruin. The outcome is absence of use and social abandonment rather than water movement.

**Rhetorical frame and outlier status.** The frame is historical warning made perceptible through ruined places. As a singleton, it supports no recurring cohort generalization and has no internal outlier.

## انبجست: water breaking out

**Eligible set:** 1 target morpheme in 1 focus ayah: 7:160.

**Window properties.** God reveals to Moses that he should strike the stone after the people request water; twelve springs then break out, and each group knows its drinking place. The focus ayah combines water distribution with shade, manna, quails, and an instruction to eat from good provision. The preceding ayat concern universal divine sovereignty, life and death, following the messenger, and a just-guiding community among Moses's people. The following ayat move to instructed settlement and forgiveness, then altered speech and punishment for wrongdoing.

**Spatial schema and outcome.** Water emerges outward from one struck stone into twelve differentiated sources, then maps onto twelve social groups. The immediate outcome is ordered, intelligible access to provision.

**Rhetorical frame and outlier status.** The frame is narrated divine provision joined to guidance, obedience, and later wrongdoing. As a singleton, it has no recurrent or outlier comparison.

## ثجاج: profusely pouring water

**Eligible set:** 1 target morpheme in 1 focus ayah: 78:14.

**Window properties.** God sends `ماء ثجاجا` down from the rain-bearing clouds. The two preceding ayat place this descent in a vertically ordered cosmos: seven strong structures above and a blazing lamp. The two following ayat state the purpose and terrestrial result: bringing out grain, vegetation, and densely interlaced gardens.

**Spatial schema and outcome.** The schema is concentrated vertical descent followed by outward biological emergence. The outcome chain is water, then grain and vegetation, then gardens.

**Rhetorical frame and outlier status.** The frame is a compact cosmological inventory of created provisions. As a singleton, it yields no cross-occurrence recurrence or internal outlier.

## سيل / سالت: flowing torrent

**Eligible set:** 3 target morphemes in 2 focus ayat: 13:17 (`سالت`, `السيل`) and 34:16 (`سيل`). The molten-copper verb at 34:12 is excluded.

**Recurring agents and actions.** In both windows, the flow originates within divine action: God sends water from the sky at 13:17 and sends the flood of the dam at 34:16. The torrent interacts forcefully with a receiving landscape. At 13:17 valleys flow according to their capacities and the flood carries raised foam; at 34:16 the flood replaces two productive gardens with two gardens of poor produce.

**Recurring spatial schema.** Both uses are terrain-bound rather than open-water scenes. Flow occupies or crosses valleys and cultivated settlement space, and its meaning is expressed through what the moving water carries away, leaves, or transforms.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** Both windows use the event to separate valued from devalued outcomes. At 13:17 transient foam is removed while what benefits people remains, in an explicit truth/falsehood parable. At 34:15-17 a good land and productive gardens become reduced vegetation after the people's refusal and are explicitly framed as recompense for disbelief.

**Subclusters and outliers.** The two windows form distinct subclusters: constructive sorting in a universal parable (13:17) and destructive historical judgment (34:16). With only two focus ayat, neither should be treated as a statistical outlier; the contrast itself is the cohort's main baseline fact.

## غيث / يغاث: rain and rain-relief

**Eligible set:** 4 target morphemes in 4 focus ayat: 12:49 (`يغاث`), 31:34, 42:28, 57:20 (`غيث`).

**Recurring agents and actions.** Rain marks a change whose timing is not under ordinary human control. It arrives after seven severe years at 12:49; God alone is presented as knowing the Hour and sending it down at 31:34; God sends it after despair at 42:28; and it initiates crop growth in the worldly-life simile at 57:20. Explicit human actions occur after or around it: people press produce (12:49), cannot know tomorrow or place of death (31:34), move from despair under measured provision to mercy (42:27-28), or admire the resulting plants before their decline (57:20).

**Recurring temporal and spatial schema.** The strongest common schema is temporal: severity, ignorance, deprivation, or bare potential precedes a divinely controlled transition, and growth, production, mercy, or a complete plant cycle follows. Spatially, descent is explicit at 31:34 and 42:28; agricultural terrestrial effect is explicit at 12:47-49 and 57:20.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** Three windows foreground provision or control of provision (12:49, 31:34, 42:26-28). Two explicitly couple the rain with relief after extremity (12:48-49, 42:28). The rhetorical frames vary: interpreted prediction (12:49), limits of human knowledge (31:34), mercy and measured provision (42:26-30), and a parable of worldly transience (57:20-22).

**Subclusters.** The relief-after-hardship subcluster is 12:49 and 42:28. Divine knowledge and exclusive control dominate 31:34. Agricultural growth followed by decay dominates 57:20.

**Outliers.** 57:20 is the only occurrence where the rain's produce is carried through yellowing and destruction, so the final point is transience rather than ongoing relief. 31:34 mentions no visible agricultural result and places rain within a list of inaccessible knowledge. 12:49 is the only passive verb form and can describe the people as receiving relief rather than naming rain directly.

## فرات: fresh, sweet water

**Eligible set:** 3 target morphemes in 3 focus ayat: 25:53, 35:12, 77:27.

**Recurring agents and actions.** God establishes or supplies the fresh water in all three windows. At 25:53 and 35:12 it is one member of a two-water contrast; at 77:27 God gives it to drink after placing high mountains. Drinking is the stable human relation: 35:12 calls its drink easy to swallow, and 77:27 uses `أسقيناكم`.

**Recurring spatial schema.** The two paired occurrences locate `فرات` in a water body set against a salty-bitter body. The third locates it in a vertical earth-provision sequence involving firm structures and high mountains. Across the set, `فرات` occupies the accessible, potable side of a larger created order.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** Potability is the repeated outcome. 25:53 and 35:12 place differing water qualities within signs of divine differentiation, power, and useful provision; 35:12 explicitly ends the benefit sequence with gratitude. At 77:25-29, fresh water is one item in a sequence of creation signs embedded in warning about the deniers.

**Subclusters.** The direct `عذب فرات` versus `ملح أجاج` pair is 25:53 and 35:12. Direct giving-to-drink is 77:27.

**Outlier.** 77:27 lacks the salty counterpart and does not describe a sea; its emphasis is direct provisioning of drinkable water within a judgment discourse.

## مطر / أمطر / ممطر: rain and raining down

**Eligible set:** 15 target morphemes in 9 focus ayat: 4:102; 7:84 (`أمطرنا`, `مطرا`); 8:32; 11:82; 15:74; 25:40 (`أمطرت`, `مطر`); 26:173 (`أمطرنا`, `مطرا`, `مطر`); 27:58 (`أمطرنا`, `مطرا`, `مطر`); 46:24 (`ممطرنا`).

**Recurring agents and actions.** In the dominant subcluster, God rains a destructive material upon a wrongdoing people. `أمطرنا عليهم` recurs at 7:84, 15:74, 26:173, and 27:58; 11:82 likewise has divine action and `عليها`. Stones are explicit at 8:32, 11:82, and 15:74, while the parallel punitive-rain constructions identify the rain as bad or as the rain of the warned at 25:40, 26:173, and 27:58.

**Recurring spatial schema.** The punitive construction is vertical and targeted: material descends from the sky or onto a people/city. The preposition `على` recurs. The action is not diffuse weather but directed incidence with a bounded recipient.

**Recurring outcomes.** Destruction, reversal, and visible aftermath dominate. Several windows pair the rain with rescue/separation, destruction of others, overturning the settlement, or an invitation to inspect the criminals' outcome. The five-ayah windows repeatedly move from rejection to rescue or separation, then rain and ruined consequence.

**Recurring rhetorical frames.** Most occurrences belong to warning narratives and retrospective signs. 7:84 and nearby 7:86 repeat `فانظروا كيف كان عاقبة`; 15:75 calls the event signs for observers; 26:174 and 27:58-60 pivot from the event to sign, faith, praise, or created provision. The root is therefore heavily associated with punitive descent in this strict Quran cohort, especially in causative Form IV.

**Subclusters.** The parallel punitive-rain narratives comprise 7:84, 11:82, 15:74, 25:40, 26:173, and 27:58. The requested punishment challenge at 8:32 invokes the same stone-from-sky schema but as quoted speech before punishment is withheld in 8:33. 4:102 is ordinary rain as a physical impediment during armed travel/prayer. 46:24 is an anticipated rain cloud that is corrected as a destructive wind.

**Outliers.** 4:102 is the only realized nonpunitive rain and functions as a practical cause of discomfort permitting weapons to be set down, while vigilance remains required. 46:24 contains no actual rain event: the people infer `هذا عارض ممطرنا`, but the next clause identifies it as the hastened punitive wind. 8:32 is a defiant request rather than narration of fulfilled rain.

## وابل: heavy downpour

**Eligible set:** 3 target morphemes in 2 focus ayat: 2:264 (one) and 2:265 (two). The two five-ayah windows overlap almost completely.

**Recurring agents and actions.** In every occurrence the downpour `أصاب` a substrate. At 2:264 it strikes a smooth rock covered by soil and leaves it bare. At 2:265 it strikes a garden on elevated ground and the garden gives double produce; the second mention states that even if a downpour does not strike it, lighter rain suffices.

**Recurring spatial schema.** Water descends onto an exposed surface. The decisive variable is the recipient's underlying constitution: thin soil over rock versus a rooted, productive garden. The downpour acts as the same external input across the paired parables while the substrate determines the result.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** The outcomes are deliberately opposite: stripping and inability to retain earnings at 2:264, versus multiplied fruit at 2:265. Both are spending parables. The surrounding window contrasts charity followed by injury, public display without faith, spending for divine pleasure and self-confirmation, and choosing good rather than defective earnings.

**Subclusters and outliers.** The two focus ayat are a designed negative/positive pair, not independent thematic subclusters. Neither is an outlier; their controlled contrast is the central cohort property.

## ودق: rain emerging through cloud

**Eligible set:** 2 target morphemes in 2 focus ayat: 24:43 and 30:48.

**Recurring agents and actions.** God configures the cloud process in both windows. Winds or clouds are moved, joined, spread, stacked, or made into pieces; then the observer sees `الودق يخرج من خلاله`. The phrase is identical in both focus ayat. Incidence is selective in both: God causes it to strike whom He wills, and 24:43 also states that it is turned away from whom He wills.

**Recurring spatial schema.** The rain becomes visible as it emerges from within and through the cloud formation. The focus is not only vertical descent but passage from a composed interior through openings to selected recipients below.

**Recurring outcomes and rhetorical frames.** Both windows present the process as a sign of divine sovereignty and control. 24:41-45 surrounds it with universal praise, divine dominion and return, alternation of night and day, visual reflection, and creation of living creatures from water. 30:46-50 surrounds it with winds as good news, mercy, ships and bounty, joy after despair, and revival of dead earth as evidence for resurrection.

**Subclusters.** 24:43 emphasizes cloud assembly, hail, lightning, selective striking/aversion, and sight. 30:48 emphasizes wind-driven spreading, human rejoicing after prior despair, mercy, and the life of the earth.

**Outliers.** Neither occurrence is an outlier because the core construction is exceptionally stable. Their main difference is outcome emphasis: potentially severe hail/lightning and visual power at 24:43, versus welcome rain, joy, mercy, and revival at 30:48.
