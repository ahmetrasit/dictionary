# batch_018 Water Secondary Resonance

## 11:82 — فَلَمَّا جَآءَ أَمْرُنَا جَعَلْنَا عَٰلِيَهَا سَافِلَهَا وَأَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهَا حِجَارَةً مِّن سِجِّيلٍ مَّنضُودٍ

### أَمْطَرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The causative verb presents stones of baked clay as a punitive downpour upon the overturned settlement.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window joins two inversions: the town's upper part is made its lower part, while ordered stones descend from above. The preceding promise that the assault will not reach Lot is followed by a warning that the same marked punishment is not far from other wrongdoers.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The night departure in `فَأَسْرِ` and the preposition `عَلَيْهَا` supply movement and exposure imagery, but neither creates a sufficiently word-local bridge from this punitive raining to another م ط ر branch.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001431/B002` is the primary punishment sense, not a secondary resonance. No explicit seeking, racing, garment, waterskin, silence, scent, or fixed-habit cue supports `root_001431/B003`–`B011`; the bare descending image does not independently activate ordinary water-rain `root_001431/B001`.

## 15:74 — فَجَعَلْنَا عَٰلِيَهَا سَافِلَهَا وَأَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهِمْ حِجَارَةً مِّن سِجِّيلٍ

### أَمْطَرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The verb depicts the sending of stones upon the people as a rain of punishment.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The stone-rain follows the dawn cry and the town's vertical reversal, then becomes a readable sign beside an enduring road rather than only a past destructive event.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `بِسَبِيلٍ مُّقِيمٍ` makes the ruined place a lasting roadside sign, but a road's presence alone does not visibly enact the going-off-across-land sense of `root_001431/B005` in the target word.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001431/B002` supplies the primary punishment sense. The window has no qualifying cue for ordinary beneficial rain, exposure, seeking, swift racing, protective clothing, scent, waterskin filling, silence, or fixed habit.

## 25:40 — وَلَقَدْ أَتَوْا۟ عَلَى ٱلْقَرْيَةِ ٱلَّتِىٓ أُمْطِرَتْ مَطَرَ ٱلسَّوْءِ أَفَلَمْ يَكُونُوا۟ يَرَوْنَهَا بَلْ كَانُوا۟ لَا يَرْجُونَ نُشُورًا

### أُمْطِرَتْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The passive verb says that the settlement was subjected to a punitive raining, specified immediately as `مَطَرَ ٱلسَّوْءِ`.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The destroyed settlement is not remote: the people have actually come upon it and can see it, yet their lack of expectation of resurrection prevents sight from becoming recognition.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B005` (going off across the land) is retained. It turns the passive catastrophe into a fixed encounter on a human route: the place that received the rain is now reached by later travelers.

**Activation trace.** `أَتَوْا۟ عَلَى ٱلْقَرْيَةِ` is the visible travel construction, reinforced by `سَبِيلًا` in 25:42 → it activates `root_001431/B005` → the abductive bridge is that movement through the land brings the observers directly across the rain-marked site → `أُمْطِرَتْ` contributes not only a past event but a punishment made available as encountered route-evidence.

**Negative evidence.** Ordinary rain `root_001431/B001` remains only the lexical precipitation image beneath the primary `root_001431/B002`; no local water or fertility contrast activates it independently. No cue meets the burden for `root_001431/B003`, `B004`, or `B006`–`B011`.

### مَطَرَ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The cognate accusative names and intensifies the punitive downpour, with `ٱلسَّوْءِ` fixing its evil character.

**Ayah-level surprise.** What fell once remains visually present to passersby; the failure lies not in absence of evidence but in seeing without expecting renewed life.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B005` (going off across the land) is retained. It gives this named evil rain an afterlife as a landmark met in travel.

**Activation trace.** `أَتَوْا۟ عَلَى ٱلْقَرْيَةِ` and the later `سَبِيلًا` visibly frame travel → they activate `root_001431/B005` → because travelers come upon the very settlement characterized by `مَطَرَ ٱلسَّوْءِ`, motion across land becomes the means by which that rain should be read → the noun now denotes both the falling punishment and its route-borne testimony.

**Negative evidence.** The modifier `ٱلسَّوْءِ` establishes primary `root_001431/B002`, but does not by itself activate ordinary rain `root_001431/B001`. There is no adequate local trigger for exposure, rain-seeking, racing, garment, scent, waterskin, silence, or fixed-habit branches.

## 26:173 — وَأَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّطَرًا فَسَآءَ مَطَرُ ٱلْمُنذَرِينَ

### أَمْطَرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The verb states that a punitive rain was sent upon those left for destruction.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The event is framed between destruction and a sign, while the following pairing of the Lord as mighty and merciful keeps rescue and punishment in the same immediate horizon.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The repetition `أَمْطَرْنَا ... مَّطَرًا ... مَطَرُ` intensifies and evaluates the same punitive event; repetition is not a visible trigger for a distinct root branch.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001431/B002` is primary. Neither the rescued exception nor the divine attributes visibly activate ordinary rain, exposure, seeking, terrestrial movement, racing, garment, scent, waterskin, silence, or fixed habit.

### مَّطَرًا — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The indefinite cognate noun supplies the substance/event rained upon them and intensifies the preceding verb.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its initially unqualified form is immediately judged by `فَسَآءَ`, so the sequence makes evaluation arrive after the compact image of a downpour.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The verb–noun repetition provides emphasis, and `فَسَآءَ` supplies explicit moral evaluation, but neither bridges the noun to another accepted م ط ر branch.

**Negative evidence.** The punishment branch `root_001431/B002` governs the primary reading. No distinct local trigger supports `root_001431/B001` or `B003`–`B011`.

### مَطَرُ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** In `مَطَرُ ٱلْمُنذَرِينَ`, the noun identifies the downpour as the dreadful rain belonging to a warned people.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The construction retrospectively classifies the event: warning becomes a concrete, completed rain and then an ayah for later hearers.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `ٱلْمُنذَرِينَ` visibly ties the rain to prior warning and confirms punishment, but it supplies no route from the target to a separate branch of م ط ر.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001431/B002` is primary. The window lacks qualifying cues for `root_001431/B001` and `B003`–`B011`.

## 27:58 — وَأَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّطَرًا فَسَآءَ مَطَرُ ٱلْمُنذَرِينَ

### أَمْطَرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The verb denotes the sending of a punitive downpour upon the warned community.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Two ayat later, descent from the sky produces water, gardens, and delight. The window therefore places destructive and life-producing descent in sharp proximity.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B001` (rainwater pouring from sky or cloud) is retained. It makes the punitive verb an inversion of the familiar life-giving descent rather than an isolated mode of destruction.

**Activation trace.** `وَأَنزَلَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً فَأَنۢبَتْنَا بِهِۦ حَدَآئِقَ` is an explicit sky-water-and-growth image → it activates `root_001431/B001` → the same downward pattern can carry either stones/punishment or water/growth → `أَمْطَرْنَا` gains a pointed reversal of rain's ordinary beneficence.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001431/B002` remains primary. The window offers no adequate trigger for exposure, seeking, land travel, racing, clothing, scent, waterskin, silence, or fixed habit.

### مَّطَرًا — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The cognate noun intensifies and concretizes the punishment sent upon them.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The nearby beneficial `مَآءً` makes the unmodified noun momentarily carry the physical shape of ordinary rain before `فَسَآءَ` condemns it.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B001` (ordinary descending rainwater) is retained. It sharpens `مَّطَرًا` as a corrupted counterpart to the sky-water that produces delightful gardens.

**Activation trace.** The visible sequence `مَّطَرًا` ... `أَنزَلَ ... مَآءً` ... `فَأَنۢبَتْنَا` → activates `root_001431/B001` → both are descending gifts of form, but their effects diverge into ruin and growth → the target noun carries an inverted-fertility dimension alongside its primary punishment sense.

**Negative evidence.** No local construction activates `root_001431/B003`–`B011`; purity in 27:56 is a description of people and does not support a rain-root branch.

### مَطَرُ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The noun in `مَطَرُ ٱلْمُنذَرِينَ` evaluates the event as the terrible rain of those who had been warned.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The condemnation is followed by praise, peace upon chosen servants, and then fertile sky-water, making the warned people's rain the negative member of a wider contrast in divine giving and judgment.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B001` (rainwater pouring from sky or cloud) is retained. It makes the evaluated `مَطَرُ` contrast directly with the beneficial descent named in 27:60.

**Activation trace.** `أَنزَلَ لَكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً` and the resulting gardens are visible local triggers → they activate `root_001431/B001` → ordinary rain's productive effect provides the comparison suppressed inside `فَسَآءَ مَطَرُ` → the target becomes specifically bad rain against a locally displayed good-rain pattern.

**Negative evidence.** Apart from primary `root_001431/B002` and retained `B001`, the window gives no qualifying evidence for `root_001431/B003`–`B011`.

## 46:24 — فَلَمَّا رَأَوْهُ عَارِضًا مُّسْتَقْبِلَ أَوْدِيَتِهِمْ قَالُوا۟ هَٰذَا عَارِضٌ مُّمْطِرُنَا بَلْ هُوَ مَا ٱسْتَعْجَلْتُم بِهِۦ رِيحٌ فِيهَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ

### مُّمْطِرُ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** In the people's speech, the active participle identifies the approaching cloud as one that will rain upon them, an expected source of water for their valleys.

**Ayah-level surprise.** `بَلْ` immediately overturns their visual interpretation: what faces their valleys is not the hoped-for rain cloud but the very thing they demanded, a wind containing painful punishment that leaves only dwellings visible.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001431/B002` (rainfall sent as punishment) is retained. It converts the speakers' expected benefaction into an ironic misrecognition of approaching chastisement while preserving their ordinary rain reading as the utterance's starting point.

**Activation trace.** `بَلْ هُوَ مَا ٱسْتَعْجَلْتُم بِهِۦ رِيحٌ فِيهَا عَذَابٌ أَلِيمٌ`, followed by `تُدَمِّرُ كُلَّ شَىْءٍ`, is the explicit correction → it activates `root_001431/B002` → the same visible cloud-form can be abductively read under the punitive branch once its contents and effect are disclosed → `مُّمْطِرُنَا` becomes tragic irony, naming desired rain at the instant punishment arrives.

**Negative evidence.** No branch beyond primary ordinary rain `root_001431/B001` and retained punishment `B002` has a qualifying trigger. `مُسْتَقْبِلَ أَوْدِيَتِهِمْ` is spatial approach, not enough for the unrelated going/racing branches, and no seeking form, protective object, waterskin, silence, scent, or fixed habit appears.

## 20:39 — أَنِ ٱقْذِفِيهِ فِى ٱلتَّابُوتِ فَٱقْذِفِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ فَلْيُلْقِهِ ٱلْيَمُّ بِٱلسَّاحِلِ يَأْخُذْهُ عَدُوٌّ لِّى وَعَدُوٌّ لَّهُۥ وَأَلْقَيْتُ عَلَيْكَ مَحَبَّةً مِّنِّى وَلِتُصْنَعَ عَلَىٰ عَيْنِىٓ

### يَمِّ — ي م م

**Primary reading.** The noun names the great body of water into which Moses, enclosed in the chest, is to be cast.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A sequence that appears to abandon the infant instead moves him under command from chest to water to shore and into the enemy's hands, all within the declared purpose of love, watchfulness, and formation.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001697/B001` (deliberately aiming toward a target) is retained. Despite the inventory's noted separation of the sea homonym, the repeated directed commands make this sea a deliberately chosen course rather than an undirected danger.

**Activation trace.** `ٱقْذِفِيهِ ... فَٱقْذِفِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ` supplies repeated imperatives with an explicit destination → it activates `root_001697/B001` → the sea is the aimed-at middle point in a commanded chain whose endpoint is the shore and divine formation → `يَمِّ` contributes a directed providential route alongside its primary water sense.

**Negative evidence.** The high-burden homonymous bridge is accepted only for `B001` because direction is overt. No dust, wiping, or prayer supports `root_001697/B002`, and no bird or proper-name cue supports `B004` or `B005`; `root_001697/B003` is primary.

### يَمُّ — ي م م

**Primary reading.** As grammatical subject, the sea is commanded to cast the chest onto the shore.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The body of water behaves as an obedient carrier: what receives the vulnerable child also delivers him precisely to a shore within a larger protective design.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001697/B001` (deliberately aiming toward a target) is retained. The sea's agency is represented as target-directed, with `بِٱلسَّاحِلِ` naming the endpoint.

**Activation trace.** `فَلْيُلْقِهِ ٱلْيَمُّ بِٱلسَّاحِلِ` is an imperative-like sequence assigning the sea a destination, reinforced by `وَلِتُصْنَعَ عَلَىٰ عَيْنِىٓ` → it activates `root_001697/B001` → the commanded endpoint bridges the unrelated intent branch to the sea's narrated action → `يَمُّ` becomes an instrument of precise conveyance, not merely surrounding water.

**Negative evidence.** No ritual-earth, bird, or place-name signal supports `root_001697/B002`, `B004`, or `B005`. The sea branch `root_001697/B003` is the primary sense.

## 20:78 — فَأَتْبَعَهُمْ فِرْعَوْنُ بِجُنُودِهِۦ فَغَشِيَهُم مِّنَ ٱلْيَمِّ مَا غَشِيَهُمْ

### يَمِّ — ي م م

**Primary reading.** The noun names the sea whose waters overwhelmed Pharaoh and his forces.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The same sea-area contains a dry path for the rescued people and an engulfing mass for their pursuers; Pharaoh's act of leading is then explicitly judged as misguidance rather than guidance.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001697/B001` (deliberately aiming or directing oneself toward something) is retained. The pursuit is intentional, but its chosen course is exposed as misdirection when it ends in the sea's covering.

**Activation trace.** `فَأَتْبَعَهُمْ فِرْعَوْنُ` is deliberate pursuit, while `وَأَضَلَّ ... وَمَا هَدَىٰ` explicitly evaluates direction → together they activate `root_001697/B001` → the pursuer aims after Moses along a path but is not truly guided → `ٱلْيَمِّ` becomes the terminal disclosure of a wrongly aimed course as well as the overwhelming water.

**Negative evidence.** The dry path `يَبَسًا` lacks dust, face/hand wiping, and prayer, so it does not meet the burden for ritual `root_001697/B002`. No bird or proper-name cue supports `B004` or `B005`; `B003` is primary.

## 20:97 — قَالَ فَٱذْهَبْ فَإِنَّ لَكَ فِى ٱلْحَيَوٰةِ أَن تَقُولَ لَا مِسَاسَ وَإِنَّ لَكَ مَوْعِدًا لَّن تُخْلَفَهُۥ وَٱنظُرْ إِلَىٰٓ إِلَٰهِكَ ٱلَّذِى ظَلْتَ عَلَيْهِ عَاكِفًا لَّنُحَرِّقَنَّهُۥ ثُمَّ لَنَنسِفَنَّهُۥ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ نَسْفًا

### يَمِّ — ي م م

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the great water into which the burned idol's remains will be scattered.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The object of sustained devotion is reduced through an emphatic two-stage sequence, burning and then complete dispersal; immediately afterward, comprehensive knowledge replaces the destroyed object as the defining divine attribute.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001697/B001` (deliberately aiming toward a target) is retained. The sea is the expressly selected endpoint of a planned, emphatic act of eradication.

**Activation trace.** `لَنُحَرِّقَنَّهُۥ ثُمَّ لَنَنسِفَنَّهُۥ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ نَسْفًا` visibly supplies intention, sequence, emphasis, and destination → it activates `root_001697/B001` → the sea is deliberately aimed at as the place that prevents the idol's reconstitution → `ٱلْيَمِّ` contributes finality of directed disposal alongside its primary water sense.

**Negative evidence.** `فَٱذْهَبْ` alone would be too broad; retention rests on the explicit emphatic sequence and destination. No ritual-earth, bird, or proper-name cue supports `root_001697/B002`, `B004`, or `B005`; `B003` is primary.

## 28:7 — وَأَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰٓ أَنْ أَرْضِعِيهِ فَإِذَا خِفْتِ عَلَيْهِ فَأَلْقِيهِ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ وَلَا تَخَافِى وَلَا تَحْزَنِىٓ إِنَّا رَآدُّوهُ إِلَيْكِ وَجَاعِلُوهُ مِنَ ٱلْمُرْسَلِينَ

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001697/B001` (deliberately aiming toward a target) is retained. The explicit command and promised outcomes make the sea a selected stage in a purposeful trajectory.

**Negative evidence.** The homonymous bridge is supported by overt direction and outcome, but nothing supports ritual `root_001697/B002`, bird `B004`, or proper-name `B005`; `root_001697/B003` remains the primary water branch.
