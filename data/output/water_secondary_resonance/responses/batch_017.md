# batch_017 Water Secondary Resonance

## 40:72 — فِى ٱلْحَمِيمِ ثُمَّ فِى ٱلنَّارِ يُسْجَرُونَ

### حَمِيمِ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The noun contributes scalding water as the medium into which the chained deniers are dragged before being subjected to the fire.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sequence `فِى ٱلْحَمِيمِ ثُمَّ فِى ٱلنَّارِ` makes the liquid no relief from fire: immersion precedes further burning, while the preceding chains and dragging make the water part of a coercive process.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `ٱلنَّارِ` and `يُسْجَرُونَ` intensify the primary heat reading, but neither supplies charcoal, smoke, rendering, kinship, fate, or another branch-specific image of ح م م.

**Negative evidence.** Fire alone is insufficient to retain root_000001/B001, since the window never presents blackness, charcoal, ash, or smoke. The remaining branches likewise lack a visible local trigger.

## 44:46 — كَغَلْىِ ٱلْحَمِيمِ

### حَمِيمِ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The word supplies boiling hot water as the comparison by which the food's boiling in the bellies is measured.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The comparison expands into a bodily sequence across the window: internal boiling is followed by dragging to the blaze and then hot water poured over the head.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `يَغْلِى فِى ٱلْبُطُونِ`, `ٱلْجَحِيمِ`, and `صُبُّوا۟ فَوْقَ رَأْسِهِۦ` reinforce boiling, bodily exposure, and punitive pouring, all within the primary hot-water contribution.

**Negative evidence.** `طَعَام` and `كَٱلْمُهْلِ` do not explicitly identify rendered fat, so root_000001/B003 is not retained. Pouring over the head does not by itself establish the bathhouse or heating vessel of root_000001/B013.

## 44:48 — ثُمَّ صُبُّوا۟ فَوْقَ رَأْسِهِۦ مِنْ عَذَابِ ٱلْحَمِيمِ

### حَمِيمِ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The word denotes the scalding water whose punishment is to be poured over the condemned person's head.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window joins three modes of forced contact: boiling within the belly, being hauled into the blaze, and an overhead pouring that turns a normally water-bearing gesture into torment.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `صُبُّوا۟ فَوْقَ رَأْسِهِۦ` makes the hot water body-directed, while `ذُقْ` adds compelled tasting; these alter the scene of punishment without visibly naming another ح م م branch.

**Negative evidence.** The overhead pour can suggest washing, but no bathhouse or heating vessel appears, so root_000001/B013 does not pass. Nor do blackness, kinship, imminence, fate, or the other inventory images receive a local trigger.

## 55:44 — يَطُوفُونَ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ حَمِيمٍ ءَانٍ

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The noun contributes intensely hot water as one of the two stations between which the criminals circulate.

**Ayah-level surprise.** `يَطُوفُونَ بَيْنَهَا وَبَيْنَ` makes the water not a single encounter but one pole of repeated movement, contrasted immediately afterward with the gardens promised to one who fears standing before his Lord.

**Water-root resonance.** root_000001/B006 (nearness, arrival, or becoming due) is retained. Its role is to make the water's heat feel as though it has arrived at its appointed, fully present condition, supplementing rather than replacing the hot-water sense.

**Activation trace.** The immediately adjacent modifier `ءَانٍ` supplies the trigger: its image of a condition having come due or reached its time closely matches B006's arrival and due-time scope. The abductive bridge is the tight noun-modifier construction `حَمِيمٍ ءَانٍ`; thus the water is read not merely as hot but as heat brought to its culminating point.

**Negative evidence.** The window gives no comparably direct cue for charcoal or smoke, rendered fat, kinship, fate, a bathhouse, or the other ح م م branches.

## 56:42 — فِى سَمُومٍ وَحَمِيمٍ

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes scalding water as part of the condition surrounding the companions of the left.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The following `ظِلٍّ` initially offers the shape of shelter, but `مِّن يَحْمُومٍ لَّا بَارِدٍ` reverses that expectation: water, wind, and shade all fail to cool.

**Water-root resonance.** root_000001/B001 (blackness, charcoal, and dark smoke) and root_000001/B004 (heat and its bodily or environmental effects) are retained. B001 darkens the hot-water image by coupling it with smoke-like black shade; B004 extends the word's heat from the liquid itself into the surrounding atmosphere and bodily exposure.

**Activation trace.** For B001, the explicit same-root form `يَحْمُومٍ` occurs in `ظِلٍّ مِّن يَحْمُومٍ`, directly realizing the branch's black smoke image; the bridge is that the target's heat belongs to the same enveloping scene as this dark false shade. For B004, `سَمُومٍ` coordinated with `حَمِيمٍ` and the denial `لَّا بَارِدٍ` visibly frame environmental heat and failed cooling; the bridge carries the target from a hot liquid to one element of total heat exposure.

**Negative evidence.** The exact B001 form and the heat/cold contrast support only those two branches. No local cue establishes rendering, kinship, fate, bathing equipment, or the remaining inventory senses.

## 56:93 — فَنُزُلٌ مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The word denotes hot water presented as the allotted reception for the denying and straying group.

**Ayah-level surprise.** `نُزُلٌ` casts the scalding water in the form of hospitality, while the preceding `سَلَٰمٌ` for the companions of the right and the following `تَصْلِيَةُ جَحِيمٍ` expose the punitive inversion.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `نُزُلٌ` changes the rhetorical role of the primary liquid into an offered reception, and the contrast with `سَلَٰمٌ` sharpens that irony. Neither cue, however, realizes another ح م م branch.

**Negative evidence.** Hospitality alone does not establish the near-kin or intimate-friend sense of root_000001/B011, because no relation or affection is present. The blaze and certainty language also do not specifically activate blackness, fate, or another branch.

## 78:25 — إِلَّا حَمِيمًا وَغَسَّاقًا

### حَمِيمًا — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The word supplies scalding water as one of the only drinks available in the place described.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The exception construction `لَا يَذُوقُونَ فِيهَا بَرْدًا وَلَا شَرَابًا إِلَّا` makes what is grammatically admitted as drink negate both refreshment and coolness; `جَزَآءً وِفَاقًا` then frames it as fitting recompense.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `بَرْدًا`, `شَرَابًا`, `يَذُوقُونَ`, and the exception visibly reshape the primary water image into anti-refreshment. `جَزَآءً` and `حِسَابًا` add a recompense frame at the ayah level.

**Negative evidence.** Negated coolness reinforces hotness but does not independently activate the sweat or summer-effects branch root_000001/B004. Recompense and reckoning are not explicit fate or death cues sufficient for root_000001/B010; all other branches are likewise untriggered.

## 4:102 — وَإِذَا كُنتَ فِيهِمْ فَأَقَمْتَ لَهُمُ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ فَلْتَقُمْ طَآئِفَةٌ مِّنْهُم مَّعَكَ وَلْيَأْخُذُوٓا۟ أَسْلِحَتَهُمْ فَإِذَا سَجَدُوا۟ فَلْيَكُونُوا۟ مِن وَرَآئِكُمْ وَلْتَأْتِ طَآئِفَةٌ أُخْرَىٰ لَمْ يُصَلُّوا۟ فَلْيُصَلُّوا۟ مَعَكَ وَلْيَأْخُذُوا۟ حِذْرَهُمْ وَأَسْلِحَتَهُمْ وَدَّ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ لَوْ تَغْفُلُونَ عَنْ أَسْلِحَتِكُمْ وَأَمْتِعَتِكُمْ فَيَمِيلُونَ عَلَيْكُم مَّيْلَةً وَٰحِدَةً وَلَا جُنَاحَ عَلَيْكُمْ إِن كَانَ بِكُمْ أَذًى مِّن مَّطَرٍ أَوْ كُنتُم مَّرْضَىٰٓ أَن تَضَعُوٓا۟ أَسْلِحَتَكُمْ وَخُذُوا۟ حِذْرَكُمْ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ أَعَدَّ لِلْكَٰفِرِينَ عَذَابًا مُّهِينًا

### مَّطَرٍ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes ordinary rain whose harmful effects can justify temporarily putting down weapons.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Rain enters a tightly ordered prayer-and-defense procedure as a bodily impediment alongside illness, yet the permission it occasions is immediately bounded by `وَخُذُوا۟ حِذْرَكُمْ` because the enemy seeks one concentrated attack.

**Water-root resonance.** root_001431/B003 (exposure to rain or open exposure) and root_001431/B005 (going off across the land) are retained. B003 makes rain an exposure condition affecting bodies and equipment; B005 locates that weather hazard within sustained movement away from home and across land.

**Activation trace.** For B003, the explicit construction `أَذًى مِّن مَّطَرٍ`, paired with arms, vigilance, and illness, visibly presents harmful exposure; the bridge is that rain matters here through contact with people standing in an exposed defensive setting. For B005, `يُهَاجِرْ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ`, `يَخْرُجْ مِنۢ بَيْتِهِۦ`, `ضَرَبْتُمْ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ`, and `ٱبْتِغَآءِ ٱلْقَوْمِ` repeatedly mark departure and land travel; the bridge makes the target weather a hazard intrinsic to going out across the land rather than an isolated shower.

**Negative evidence.** The enemy's `مَيْلَةً وَٰحِدَةً` is an attack, but no horse, rider, bird, racing, or other branch-specific motion cue warrants root_001431/B006. No rain garment, waterskin, silence, or fixed-habit image appears.

## 7:84 — وَأَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّطَرًا فَٱنظُرْ كَيْفَ كَانَ عَٰقِبَةُ ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ

### أَمْطَرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The causative verb presents the shower as deliberately sent down upon the people in punishment.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding rescue of one household except the wife makes the shower discriminate within the narrative sequence, and `فَٱنظُرْ ... عَٰقِبَةُ` turns the event into an outcome to be inspected.

**Water-root resonance.** root_001431/B001 (rainwater pouring from sky or cloud) is retained. It preserves the material shower beneath the verb's punitive causation, so the punishment is imagined as an actual rain-pattern descent rather than only an abstract calamity.

**Activation trace.** The verb takes the explicit same-root object `مَّطَرًا` in `أَمْطَرْنَا عَلَيْهِم مَّطَرًا`. That cognate-object construction is the visible trigger for B001; the abductive bridge is that naming the shower itself foregrounds the ordinary physical rain pattern inside the causative punishment sense.

**Negative evidence.** `أَخْرِجُوهُم مِّن قَرْيَتِكُمْ` supplies expulsion language, but it does not establish the independent land-ranging motion of root_001431/B005. No specific cue supports exposure, rain-seeking, racing, a garment, or the higher-burden isolated senses.

### مَّطَرًا — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The noun names the descending shower that functions here as punishment upon the criminals.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The same rescue-and-exception sequence makes this rain an instrument with a sharply selective narrative outcome rather than a general weather event.

**Water-root resonance.** root_001431/B002 (rainfall sent as punishment) is retained. It assigns the noun an explicitly punitive role, changing `مَّطَرًا` from bare precipitation into a shower whose manner and consequence are chastisement.

**Activation trace.** The immediately governing same-root form-IV verb `أَمْطَرْنَا`, the directional `عَلَيْهِم`, and the result `عَٰقِبَةُ ٱلْمُجْرِمِينَ` jointly trigger B002. The abductive bridge is that causative sending upon identified criminals specifies why this otherwise rain-denoting noun falls.

**Negative evidence.** Being rained upon is inherent in the primary event and is not enough by itself to retain the separate exposure branch root_001431/B003. The window lacks branch-specific cues for seeking, racing, a waterskin, silence, habit, or a rain garment.

## 8:32 — وَإِذْ قَالُوا۟ ٱللَّهُمَّ إِن كَانَ هَٰذَا هُوَ ٱلْحَقَّ مِنْ عِندِكَ فَأَمْطِرْ عَلَيْنَا حِجَارَةً مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ أَوِ ٱئْتِنَا بِعَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ

### أَمْطِرْ — م ط ر

**Primary reading.** The imperative asks God to rain stones upon the speakers as painful punishment if the recited message is true.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Their challenge converts claimed certainty into a self-directed demand for proof by destruction; the next ayah answers by naming conditions under which punishment is withheld.

**Water-root resonance.** root_001431/B001 (rainwater pouring from sky or cloud) and root_001431/B004 (seeking rain or rainlike good) are retained. B001 supplies the physical template by which stones are imagined descending like rain; B004 turns the utterance into a perverse rain-invocation that petitions for harm instead of benefit.

**Activation trace.** For B001, `عَلَيْنَا حِجَارَةً مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ` visibly preserves the downward source, recipients, and shower construction while replacing water with stones; the bridge is analogical material descent. For B004, the vocative-request sequence `ٱللَّهُمَّ ... فَأَمْطِرْ عَلَيْنَا` directly addresses God and asks for the shower; the branch's rain-seeking role is activated, but `حِجَارَةً` and `عَذَابٍ أَلِيمٍ` invert the expected sought good into demanded punishment.

**Negative evidence.** The surrounding plotting, killing, expulsion, and barring from the sanctuary do not specifically realize land-ranging or swift racing branches. The isolated fragrance, waterskin, silence, and fixed-habit senses have no local trigger.
