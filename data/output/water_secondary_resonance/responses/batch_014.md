# batch_014 Water Secondary Resonance

## 25:49 — لِّنُحْۦِىَ بِهِۦ بَلْدَةً مَّيْتًا وَنُسْقِيَهُۥ مِمَّا خَلَقْنَآ أَنْعَٰمًا وَأَنَاسِىَّ كَثِيرًا

### نُسْقِيَ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The form-IV verb presents the descended pure water as something God supplies for the drinking of cattle and many humans, alongside its revival of a dead town.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window aligns `ٱلنَّوْمَ سُبَاتًا / ٱلنَّهَارَ نُشُورًا` with `بَلْدَةً مَّيْتًا / لِّنُحْۦِىَ`, then follows the distributed water with remembrance and refusal (`لِيَذَّكَّرُوا۟` / `كُفُورًا`). The supply of drink is therefore also one element in a repeated life-restoration sign, not merely relief of thirst.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B003` (the water-share of land and irrigation) is retained as a landward resonance. It makes `نُسْقِيَ` participate in sustaining the inhabited environment from which creaturely drinking cannot be detached.

**Activation trace.** The visible sequence is water descending from the sky (`أَنزَلْنَا ... مَآءً`), reviving `بَلْدَةً مَّيْتًا`, and then being apportioned `بَيْنَهُمْ` in 25:50. Those cues supply land, watering, and distribution; abductively, the same rain supplied to drinkers also constitutes a territory's irrigation share. Thus B003 changes the target from a discrete delivery to cattle and people into part of a distributed land-and-life system.

**Negative evidence.** The winds and descending water do not name a cloud, so B007 is not retained. There is no prayer formula for B006, crop/channel detail for a narrower channel reading, bodily fluid, reed, dye, or hostile speech to activate B005 or B008-B010.

## 26:79 — وَٱلَّذِى هُوَ يُطْعِمُنِى وَيَسْقِينِ

### يَسْقِي — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb says that the speaker's Lord gives him drink, paired directly with giving him food.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Creation and guidance precede the food-drink pair, while illness/healing and death/life follow it. Ordinary drinking is thereby placed inside a continuous series of divine acts that spans bodily maintenance, recovery, and existence itself.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `يُطْعِمُنِى وَيَسْقِينِ` strongly realizes ordinary giving of food and drink, but the neighboring acts expand the ayah at the level of divine care rather than visibly activating a different branch of س ق ي.

**Negative evidence.** `مَرِضْتُ` is too general to activate `root_000722/B005`, which specifically requires abnormal enclosed belly fluid. No water source or allotment, land, prayer for rain, cloud, reed, dye, or repeated hostile speech is visible.

## 28:24 — فَسَقَىٰ لَهُمَا ثُمَّ تَوَلَّىٰٓ إِلَى ٱلظِّلِّ فَقَالَ رَبِّ إِنِّى لِمَآ أَنزَلْتَ إِلَىَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ

### سَقَىٰ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** He performs the two women's immediate watering task for them, a concrete act of service at the water of Midian.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Immediately after supplying their need, he withdraws and declares himself poor toward whatever good his Lord sends down; the next ayah makes the completed service the ground of a summons and recompense. The provider of water is thus simultaneously exposed as a recipient in need.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B002` (providing a watering source or access to it) is retained. It recasts the act from doing one task into making a blocked communal resource usable for the two women.

**Activation trace.** In 28:23 the setting is explicitly `مَآءَ مَدْيَنَ`; a crowd is watering, the women are holding back, and they say they cannot water until the shepherds depart. That congestion is a visible access problem. Abductively, his `فَسَقَىٰ لَهُمَا` resolves not only thirst but exclusion from the source, so B002 adds restored access to the primary act.

**Negative evidence.** The prayer beginning `رَبِّ` asks for `خَيْرٍ`, not water or rain, so it does not meet the burden for B006. No land/crop/channel, bodily fluid, rain cloud, reed, dye, or hostile-heart construction activates B003 or B005-B010.

## 28:25 — فَجَآءَتْهُ إِحْدَىٰهُمَا تَمْشِى عَلَى ٱسْتِحْيَآءٍ قَالَتْ إِنَّ أَبِى يَدْعُوكَ لِيَجْزِيَكَ أَجْرَ مَا سَقَيْتَ لَنَا فَلَمَّا جَآءَهُۥ وَقَصَّ عَلَيْهِ ٱلْقَصَصَ قَالَ لَا تَخَفْ نَجَوْتَ مِنَ ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلظَّٰلِمِينَ

### سَقَيْ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** `مَا سَقَيْتَ لَنَا` identifies his completed watering service for the two women as the act for which their father intends to repay him.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The small water service becomes `أَجْر`, then prompts the assessment `ٱلْقَوِىُّ ٱلْأَمِينُ` and an employment proposal. The act's contribution exceeds the water delivered: it publicly discloses character and opens a durable social arrangement.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B002` (providing a watering source or access) is retained. The recalled act carries the additional sense that he enabled the women to use a source from which the crowd had effectively held them back.

**Activation trace.** The window begins with `مَآءَ مَدْيَنَ`, the watering crowd, the women's `تَذُودَانِ`, and their inability to water until the shepherds leave. The focus phrase then converts that access-restoring deed into a compensated service. The abductive bridge is that overcoming the source barrier amounts to providing usable watering access; B002 therefore makes `سَقَيْتَ` socially enabling, not only manually helpful.

**Negative evidence.** Neither payment nor employment activates another water sense. There is no rain prayer, cultivated land or channel, bodily fluid, cloud, reed, dye, or hostile speech for B003 or B005-B010.

## 76:17 — وَيُسْقَوْنَ فِيهَا كَأْسًا كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا زَنجَبِيلًا

### يُسْقَ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The passive verb presents the inhabitants as being served a cup whose mixture is ginger.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The cup is framed by silver vessels and precisely measured glassware before it, a named spring after it, and circulating attendants after that. Drinking appears as an ordered environment of prepared abundance rather than an isolated beverage.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B002` (provision of a watering source) is retained. It extends the served cup into continuing provision grounded in a spring within the same realm.

**Activation trace.** The immediate continuation `عَيْنًا فِيهَا تُسَمَّىٰ سَلْسَبِيلًا` visibly supplies a resident spring, while `وَيُطَافُ عَلَيْهِم` and the vessels establish service. Abductively, the cup is not a one-off draught but an offered drink backed by an available source; B002 changes the target from cup-service alone to source-secured provision.

**Negative evidence.** `مِزَاجُهَا` names a beverage mixture, not cloth saturated with dye, so B009 is not activated. Nothing indicates agricultural irrigation, belly fluid, prayer for rain, a rain cloud, reed growth, or hostile speech.

## 83:25 — يُسْقَوْنَ مِن رَّحِيقٍ مَّخْتُومٍ

### يُسْقَ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The passive verb says that they are served from a sealed pure drink.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Facial radiance precedes the drink; its seal, musk, invitation to compete, and blend from Tasnim follow it. The serving therefore marks recognized felicity and an object of aspiration, not merely consumption.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The seal, scent, competition, and mixture richly qualify the beverage and its status, but none supplies the defining construction of another س ق ي branch.

**Negative evidence.** `مِزَاجُهُۥ مِن تَسْنِيمٍ` gives the blend's provenance but does not visibly establish an at-will watering source or allotment for B002. There is no land/channel, bodily fluid, rain prayer or cloud, reed, dyed cloth, or forced hostile speech.

## 88:5 — تُسْقَىٰ مِنْ عَيْنٍ ءَانِيَةٍ

### تُسْقَىٰ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The passive verb depicts the suffering figure as made to drink from a fiercely hot spring.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A blazing fire precedes the hot spring, and food that neither fattens nor relieves hunger follows it. Drink and food retain their ordinary bodily roles only to reverse them: the whole sustenance sequence intensifies deprivation.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `نَارًا حَامِيَةً`, `عَيْنٍ ءَانِيَةٍ`, and the nutritionally useless food create a strong punitive contrast, but this remains an ayah-level inversion of nourishment rather than a separately grounded س ق ي branch.

**Negative evidence.** Although `عَيْنٍ` is a source, coerced drinking from a harmful spring is not the usable, at-will provision required by B002. B010 would require disliked words or hostility repeatedly forced into the heart; no speech, heart, or repetition appears. The window also lacks land irrigation, belly fluid, prayer, cloud, reed, and dye.

## 91:13 — فَقَالَ لَهُمْ رَسُولُ ٱللَّهِ نَاقَةَ ٱللَّهِ وَسُقْيَٰهَا

### سُقْيَٰ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the she-camel's protected watering entitlement or allotted access, coupled with the warning to leave God's she-camel inviolate.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The compact warning is immediately followed by denial, hamstringing, and comprehensive punishment. Respect for a creature's water entitlement becomes the concrete boundary whose violation exposes the community's wider rebellion.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B001` (giving drink to a drinker) is retained. It keeps the allotted `سُقْيَٰهَا` tied to the actual act of satisfying a living animal's need.

**Activation trace.** The explicit `نَاقَةَ` immediately precedes the coordinated possessive `وَسُقْيَٰهَا`; the drinker and her watering are thus visibly joined. Abductively, an allotment matters because it must issue in the camel actually receiving drink. B001 changes the target from an abstract schedule or right into embodied care for its beneficiary.

**Negative evidence.** No cultivated land, crop, or channel supports B003, and there is no prayer formula for B006. The window supplies no belly fluid, cloud, reed, dye, or hostile-heart idiom.

## 7:64 — فَكَذَّبُوهُ فَأَنجَيْنَٰهُ وَٱلَّذِينَ مَعَهُۥ فِى ٱلْفُلْكِ وَأَغْرَقْنَا ٱلَّذِينَ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَٰتِنَآ إِنَّهُمْ كَانُوا۟ قَوْمًا عَمِينَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** God causes those who denied the signs to drown, in direct contrast to saving the messenger and those with him in the ark.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding appeal presents warning, guardedness, and hoped-for mercy; the focus ayah then resolves response into the sharp spatial opposition of safety `فِى ٱلْفُلْكِ` and drowning. The next ayah opens another people's address, so the event also closes one failed reception and clears the narrative field for another.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) is retained. It makes the causative drowning not merely submersion but the terminal execution of judgment upon the deniers.

**Activation trace.** The same clause contrasts causative rescue (`فَأَنجَيْنَٰهُ`) with causative drowning (`وَأَغْرَقْنَا`) and identifies the latter group twice by denial. Because rescue and its lethal opposite partition the two groups, the abductive bridge to B002 is explicit intentional death by water. The branch makes finality and judicial discrimination part of the target's contribution.

**Negative evidence.** No tear-filled eye or saturated land activates B003, no bow activates B004, and no whole-scope absorption language activates B005. The later `ٱلْمَلَأُ` is a social designation in a new episode, not an image of an eye or land filled with water; milk-draught and ornamental-bridle branches also lack triggers.

## 7:136 — فَٱنتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ فَأَغْرَقْنَٰهُمْ فِى ٱلْيَمِّ بِأَنَّهُمْ كَذَّبُوا۟ بِـَٔايَٰتِنَا وَكَانُوا۟ عَنْهَا غَٰفِلِينَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** God causes them to drown in the great water as vengeance for denying and neglecting the signs.

**Ayah-level surprise.** They first promise faith and release if the affliction is removed, then break that promise. After their drowning, the formerly weakened people inherit the eastern and western lands, the old works are destroyed, and they cross `ٱلْبَحْرَ`; the water event thus becomes a reversal of mastery, barring one group where another passes onward.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) and `root_001080/B005` (whole-scope immersion or absorption) are retained. B002 makes the drowning the deliberately lethal act of vengeance; B005 makes it the pivot of a comprehensive removal and transfer, not only the loss of bodies.

**Activation trace.** For B002, `فَٱنتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ فَأَغْرَقْنَٰهُمْ` directly links intentional vengeance to causative drowning, while `بِأَنَّهُمْ` supplies the stated ground. For B005, 7:137 visibly totalizes the aftermath with `مَشَٰرِقَ ٱلْأَرْضِ وَمَغَٰرِبَهَا` and `دَمَّرْنَا مَا كَانَ يَصْنَعُ ... وَمَا كَانُوا۟ يَعْرِشُونَ`. Abductively, the drowning opens into the absorption of the former order's entire territorial and constructed scope; this adds regime-wide displacement to the primary water death.

**Negative evidence.** No eye filled with tears or land saturated by irrigation supports B003, and there is no bow, small milk draught, or ornamented bridle for B004, B006, or B008.

### يَمِّ — ي م م

**Primary reading.** `ٱلْيَمِّ` denotes the sea or great body of water in which the drowning occurs.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The following window calls the crossed water `ٱلْبَحْرَ`: the same narrative span makes great water the place of one group's destruction and another group's successful passage. The yamm is therefore a boundary that discriminates outcomes, not neutral scenery.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The vengeance sequence makes the drowning purposeful, but purpose belongs to `فَٱنتَقَمْنَا` and the causative `فَأَغْرَقْنَٰهُمْ`; it does not visibly turn the noun `ٱلْيَمِّ` into an act of aiming. The sea/great-water branch remains primary without supplementation.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001697/B001` is explicitly distinct in origin from the sea branch and lacks a form or construction of قصد, تعمد, seeking, or singling out; general intentionality cannot meet that higher burden. There is no clean earth, face/hand wiping, or prayer context for B002, and no bird or Yamamah proper-name cue for B004-B005.
