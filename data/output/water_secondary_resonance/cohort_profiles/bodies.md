# Water-body cohort profiles

## Scope and method

This is a neutral descriptive baseline, not a secondary-resonance analysis. It uses only the four named root packets and their local QAC ayah contexts. Counts distinguish rooted morphemes from distinct focus ayahs. `بحيرة` and `تيمم` are excluded as requested. A recurring property below describes the primary discourse setting of an attested water word; it is not evidence that another branch of the root is active.

## ماء (`root_001458`)

### Eligible set

- **63 eligible morphemes in 60 focus ayahs.** The three extra morphemes are the second `ماء` tokens in 11:44, 47:15, and 67:30.
- **Focus refs:** 2:22, 2:74, 2:164, 4:43, 5:6, 6:99, 7:50, 7:57, 8:11, 10:24, 11:7, 11:43, 11:44, 13:4, 13:14, 13:17, 14:16, 14:32, 15:22, 16:10, 16:65, 18:29, 18:41, 18:45, 20:53, 21:30, 22:5, 22:63, 23:18, 24:39, 24:45, 25:48, 25:54, 27:60, 28:23, 29:63, 30:24, 31:10, 32:8, 32:27, 35:27, 39:21, 41:39, 43:11, 47:15, 50:9, 54:11, 54:12, 54:28, 56:31, 56:68, 67:30, 69:11, 72:16, 77:20, 77:27, 78:14, 79:31, 80:25, 86:6.

### Recurring properties

**Agents and actions.** God is the dominant controlling agent: water is sent or brought down, driven, poured, brought out, lodged in the earth, supplied for drinking, or made the material of creation. Sky-water repeatedly produces vegetation, fruit, pasture, gardens, and revived land. Humans are usually recipients: they drink, wash, irrigate, seek, distribute, or fail to obtain it. In the flood passages water becomes grammatically or narratively forceful: it rises beyond bounds, meets water from another source, surrounds, separates, and drowns.

**Spatial schemas.** The most stable schema is **above -> below -> earth/interior -> emergence**: water descends from the sky, enters or is lodged in the ground, and reappears as springs, crops, fruit, or greenness. A second schema is **source -> human access**: water is found, drunk, poured, apportioned, withdrawn below reach, mistaken at a distance, or denied across an eschatological boundary. The flood reverses controlled descent into **excess and enclosure**. Reproductive uses place water inside a generative body rather than in the sky-earth cycle.

**Outcomes.** The dominant outcomes are life, growth, food, purification, and evidence of divine provision. The revived-earth sequence frequently supports an argument about resurrection. The same noun can also carry destructive or punitive outcomes when qualified or placed in a flood/hell setting. Scarcity and failed access reveal dependence: no water for ablution, a garden's water gone deep, a mirage, an unreachable mouth, or a vanished accessible supply.

**Rhetorical frames.** Major frames are creation/provision signs, revived-earth proofs, direct questions calling for perception or gratitude, worldly-life parables, ritual law, flood narrative, and Paradise/Hell contrast. The noun is therefore semantically general but rhetorically highly structured by source, modifier, recipient, and result.

### Meaningful subclusters

- **Rain-to-life/provision:** the largest cluster. Typical chain: divine sending from sky -> earth receives -> plants/fruit/pasture emerge -> sign, gratitude, or resurrection inference. Clear examples include 2:22, 2:164, 6:99, 7:57, 14:32, 16:65, 20:53, 27:60, 29:63, 30:24, 35:27, 39:21, 41:39, 43:11, and 50:9.
- **Measured storage and accessible supply:** 15:22, 23:18, 54:28, 56:68, 67:30, 72:16, 77:27, 79:31, and 80:25 foreground giving, storing, allotting, drinking, abundance, withdrawal, or emergence.
- **Purification/ritual availability:** 4:43 and 5:6 define the absence of water as the condition for dry ablution; 8:11 and 25:48 make descending water purifying.
- **Life-material/reproductive fluid:** 21:30, 24:45, 25:54, 32:8, 77:20, and 86:6 connect water with living beings, human descent, or emitted reproductive fluid. These are not rain scenes.
- **Flood and destructive excess:** 11:43-44, 54:11-12, and 69:11 place water in the Noah narrative. The normal controlled source-recipient relation becomes overwhelming, then is decisively stopped or absorbed.
- **Eschatological drink/setting:** 7:50, 14:16, 18:29, 47:15, and 56:31 contrast denied, corrupt, scalding, unchanged, or freely poured water. Modifiers and destination determine beneficent versus punitive force.
- **Parable and false access:** 10:24 and 18:45 liken worldly life to rain-fed growth that vanishes; 13:14 and 24:39 picture water desired but never reaching the seeker.

### Outliers and controls

- **11:7** is the sole cosmological statement of the Throne being upon water and does not share the ordinary descent/growth chain.
- **28:23** uses `ماء مدين` as a socially organized watering place; the scene concerns arrival, queueing, livestock, and unequal access.
- **13:4** stresses one water producing differentiated crops, unlike the more usual water-versus-dead-land contrast.
- **47:15** is internally contrastive: one ayah has both unchanging Paradise water and scalding punitive water.
- **11:44, 54:12, and 69:11** treat flood water as a mass to be swallowed, diminished, joined, or said to have exceeded bounds; they should not be generalized from ordinary rain language.

### Cohort baseline

- `cohort=maa; packet=root_001458; eligible_morphemes=63; focus_ayahs=60`
- `dominant_agent=God; dominant_action=send/control/supply; dominant_recipient=earth+humans`
- `dominant_schema=sky_to_earth_to_growth; secondary_schemas=source_to_access|fluid_to_generation|excess_to_enclosure`
- `dominant_outcomes=life|growth|provision|purification|sign; contrast_outcomes=deprivation|deception|punishment|drowning`
- `major_frames=creation_sign|resurrection_proof|parable|ritual_law|flood_narrative|afterlife_contrast`
- `high_value_outliers=11:7|13:4|28:23|47:15`

## بحر (`root_000086`, excluding `بحيرة`)

### Eligible set

- **41 eligible morphemes in 39 focus ayahs.** The repeated focus tokens are two `بحر` words in 18:109 and `البحر` plus `أبحر` in 31:27. The `بحيرة` token at 5:103 is excluded.
- **Focus refs:** 2:50, 2:164, 5:96, 6:59, 6:63, 6:97, 7:138, 7:163, 10:22, 10:90, 14:32, 16:14, 17:66, 17:67, 17:70, 18:60, 18:61, 18:63, 18:79, 18:109, 20:77, 22:65, 24:40, 25:53, 26:63, 27:61, 27:63, 30:41, 31:27, 31:31, 35:12, 42:32, 44:24, 45:12, 52:6, 55:19, 55:24, 81:6, 82:3.

### Recurring properties

**Agents and actions.** God repeatedly subjugates, parts, holds, mixes/releases, separates, guides through, or enables travel upon the sea. Humans cross it, travel by ship, fish, eat, extract ornaments, seek bounty, work, become endangered, pray, and are rescued or drowned. Ships characteristically run `في البحر`; the sea is both a productive medium and a field in which divine control becomes visible.

**Spatial schemas.** The most frequent schema is **a wide surface carrying ships**, often paired with movement and benefit. A second is the explicit **land/sea totality** (`البر والبحر`), used for knowledge, danger, guidance, transport, or corruption. The Exodus cluster turns the expanse into a **divinely opened path** and then a closed or restored drowning medium. Dual-sea passages emphasize **meeting plus retained difference**, with a barrier or contrast of fresh and salt water. Depth appears most strongly in 24:40, where wave and cloud layers block sight.

**Outcomes.** Ordinary outcomes are mobility, food, ornament, commerce/bounty, and gratitude. Peril produces exclusive prayer and rescue; the Exodus sea produces deliverance for one group and drowning for another. In figurative or cosmic frames, the sea supplies an image of inexhaustible quantity, layered darkness, corruption across the inhabited world, or end-time destabilization.

**Rhetorical frames.** Recurring frames are signs of subjugated creation, calls to gratitude, human dependence exposed at sea, Moses/Pharaoh narrative, legal provision, paired-water demonstration, analogies of divine words, and eschatological oath/transformation.

### Meaningful subclusters

- **Navigation, ships, and provision:** 2:164, 14:32, 16:14, 17:66, 17:70, 18:79, 22:65, 31:31, 35:12, 42:32, 45:12, and 55:24 repeatedly join sea, ships, divine command/subjugation, bounty, work, or gratitude. 5:96 supplies the related legal food/fishing case.
- **Peril, navigation, rescue, and exposed devotion:** 6:63, 6:97, 10:22, 17:67, and 27:63 associate sea darkness or danger with guidance, prayer, rescue, and subsequent human response.
- **Exodus crossing and drowning:** 2:50, 7:138, 10:90, 20:77, 26:63, and 44:24 present crossing, a dry path or parted masses, and Pharaoh's drowning. The sea functions first as opened boundary/path and then as instrument of judgment.
- **Two seas, meeting, and differentiation:** 25:53, 27:61, 35:12, and 55:19 foreground duality, contact, barriers, and/or different water qualities. 18:60 belongs formally to the dual/confluence group but narratively initiates Moses's search journey rather than a water-quality proof.
- **Land/sea comprehensive domain:** 6:59, 6:63, 6:97, 10:22, 17:70, 27:63, and 30:41 use the binary to cover terrestrial and maritime reach.
- **Scale as analogy:** 18:109 and 31:27 make the sea ink or replenishing material whose apparent vastness is still insufficient for divine words.
- **Cosmic/end-time sea:** 52:6, 81:6, and 82:3 place sea(s) in oath or end-time scenes of filling/kindling and bursting open.

### Outliers and controls

- **7:163** is a shore-town legal/testing narrative; the sea supplies location and fish behavior rather than travel or danger.
- **18:61 and 18:63** focus on the fish taking a remarkable path in the sea; together with 18:60 they form a local narrative marker of the meeting place.
- **24:40** is a parable of layered darkness in a deep sea, not an actual voyage report.
- **30:41** extends corruption across land and sea without describing water behavior.
- **52:6** is an oath phrase (`البحر المسجور`) and should remain distinct from the explicit end-time clauses in 81:6 and 82:3.

### Cohort baseline

- `cohort=bahr; packet=root_000086; exclusions=bahira@5:103; eligible_morphemes=41; focus_ayahs=39`
- `dominant_agent=God; dominant_actions=subjugate|part|guide|enable_travel; human_actions=sail|fish|extract|pray|cross`
- `dominant_schema=wide_surface_carries_ships; secondary_schemas=land_sea_totality|opened_path_then_drowning|two_bodies_with_boundary|depth_layers`
- `dominant_outcomes=mobility|provision|bounty|gratitude; contrast_outcomes=peril|deliverance|drowning|cosmic_disruption`
- `major_frames=creation_sign|dependency_at_sea|exodus_narrative|paired_water_proof|quantity_analogy|eschatology`
- `high_value_outliers=5:96|7:163|18:60-63|24:40|30:41|52:6`

## يم (`root_001697`, excluding `تيمم`)

### Eligible set

- **8 eligible morphemes in 7 focus ayahs.** There are two `يم` tokens in 20:39. The three `تيمم` occurrences at 2:267, 4:43, and 5:6 are excluded.
- **Focus refs:** 7:136, 20:39, 20:78, 20:97, 28:7, 28:40, 51:40.

### Recurring properties

**Agents and actions.** Every focus ayah belongs to the Moses-Pharaoh story world. The dominant action is forceful placement **into** the `يم`: infant Moses is put/cast into it under divine instruction; Pharaoh and his forces are cast into it by God; the calf is pulverized and scattered into it. In 20:39 the `يم` itself receives the casket and is commanded/predicted to cast it onto the shore. In 20:78 it covers Pharaoh's forces.

**Spatial schemas.** The invariant prepositional schema is **motion into an encompassing water body** (`في اليم`). The preservation subcluster adds **into water -> onto shore -> recovery**, whereas the judgment subcluster ends at **into water -> covering/drowning/no return**. The calf scene uses the water as a terminal dispersal destination.

**Outcomes.** The same named water domain preserves Moses while destroying Pharaoh and erasing the material cult object. The result is not a generic seascape: the `يم` is consistently a destination to which vulnerable, guilty, or rejected entities are deliberately consigned.

**Rhetorical frames.** The dominant frames are providential rescue, retributive judgment, compressed retrospective reminder, and destruction of idolatry. Repetitions at 28:40 and 51:40 are especially formulaic (`أخذناه وجنوده فنبذناهم في اليم`).

### Meaningful subclusters

- **Infant preservation:** 20:39 and 28:7 combine fear, divine instruction, casting into the water, promised protection/return, and Moses's future role. Only 20:39 personifies the water body as the immediate agent that casts the casket onto shore.
- **Pharaoh and army destroyed:** 7:136, 20:78, 28:40, and 51:40 use drowning, covering, seizing, and casting. The water is the terminal arena of a judgment explicitly tied to denial, wrongdoing, or blame.
- **Calf disposed:** 20:97 ends the physical idol's status by burning it and scattering it into the `يم`.

### Outliers and controls

- **20:97** is the only occurrence whose patient is neither Moses nor Pharaoh's people and whose outcome is dispersal rather than rescue/drowning.
- **20:39** contains both eligible tokens and is the only ayah in which `اليم` is syntactically assigned an action toward the shore.
- **28:7** promises return before narrating any shore action, so its profile is protection under command rather than an explicit description of the water's behavior.

### Cohort baseline

- `cohort=yamm; packet=root_001697; exclusions=tayammum@2:267|4:43|5:6; eligible_morphemes=8; focus_ayahs=7`
- `narrative_domain=Moses_Pharaoh_only; dominant_action=deliberate_casting_into_water`
- `dominant_schema=into_encompassing_water; preservation_variant=into_to_shore_to_return; judgment_variant=into_to_covering_no_return`
- `dominant_outcomes=preserve_chosen|destroy_enemy|erase_idol`
- `major_frames=providence|retributive_judgment|retrospective_warning|idol_destruction`
- `high_value_outliers=20:39|20:97`

## موج / يموج (`root_001455`)

### Eligible set

- **7 eligible morphemes in 6 focus ayahs.** There are two `موج` tokens in 24:40. Six tokens are nouns (`موج`) and one is the verb `يموج` at 18:99.
- **Focus refs:** 10:22, 11:42, 11:43, 18:99, 24:40, 31:32.

### Recurring properties

**Agents and actions.** In water scenes, waves come upon people, cover them, carry ships within them, stand between people, and layer above one another. Humans are chiefly patients: surrounded, covered, separated, unable to see, frightened into prayer, rescued, or drowned. The verb at 18:99 transfers the same turbulent interpenetration to human masses: some surge within/against others before all are gathered.

**Spatial schemas.** The cohort strongly prefers **overwhelming from above or around**. 10:22 says waves come from every place; 31:32 compares them to canopies; 11:42 compares them to mountains. In 24:40 repeated `من فوقه` builds a vertical stack of deep sea -> wave -> wave -> cloud -> compounded darkness. In 11:43 a wave occupies the space **between** father and son. At 18:99 people move **in one another**, replacing discrete water layers with an intermingled mass.

**Outcomes.** Wave scenes remove normal control and visibility. They expose dependence through sincere prayer, interrupt human plans or relationships, and lead either to rescue or drowning. The parabolic sea produces epistemic darkness, while the non-water verbal case produces collective disorder immediately before final gathering.

**Rhetorical frames.** Frames include peril-at-sea signs, Noah flood narrative, a parable of compounded darkness/lack of divine light, and eschatological crowd turbulence. Height similes (`كالجبال`, `كالظلل`) and enclosure language make magnitude and loss of control unusually consistent across the small cohort.

### Meaningful subclusters

- **Sea peril exposing devotion:** 10:22 and 31:32 share sudden wave enclosure, exclusive prayer to God, promised or actual rescue, and an ensuing test of gratitude/constancy.
- **Noah flood:** 11:42-43 uses mountain-like waves as the vessel's medium and then as the barrier that ends Noah's appeal to his son.
- **Layered darkness parable:** 24:40 doubles `موج` and stacks it with deep sea and cloud; obscuration rather than voyage or drowning is the stated result.
- **Human mass turbulence:** 18:99 is the sole verbal and non-water use; it retains intermingling/instability but changes the moving medium from water to people.

### Outliers and controls

- **18:99** is the sole non-water realization and must not be counted as literal hydrology.
- **24:40** supplies two of the seven tokens and is the only deliberately doubled, vertically layered construction.
- **11:43** is the only case where a wave's primary narrative function is interposition between two named people.

### Cohort baseline

- `cohort=mawj; packet=root_001455; eligible_morphemes=7; focus_ayahs=6; noun_tokens=6; verb_tokens=1`
- `dominant_patient=humans_or_ship; dominant_actions=come_upon|cover|surround|interpose|intermingle`
- `dominant_schema=over_and_around_enclosure; secondary_schemas=between_people|vertical_layering|people_in_people`
- `dominant_outcomes=loss_of_control|fear_prayer|separation|obscuration|drowning_or_rescue`
- `major_frames=sea_peril_sign|Noah_narrative|darkness_parable|eschatological_turbulence`
- `high_value_outliers=11:43|18:99|24:40`
