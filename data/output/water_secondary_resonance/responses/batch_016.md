# batch_016 Water Secondary Resonance

## 26:120 — ثُمَّ أَغْرَقْنَا بَعْدُ ٱلْبَاقِينَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** The causative verb says that, after rescuing Noah and those with him, God drowned the people who remained.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding request to separate the two parties (فَٱفْتَحْ بَيْنِى وَبَيْنَهُمْ) is realized spatially: one party is carried in the laden ark, while the complementary party is left to the water. Drowning thus completes a prior act of rescue and separation.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) and `root_001080/B005` (taking over an entire field) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_001080/B002`, وَنَجِّنِى and فَأَنجَيْنَٰهُ supply the explicit survival pole, while ٱلْبَاقِينَ supplies its doomed complement; the bridge is that the causative drowning is the lethal disposition of the non-rescued party, so أَغْرَقْنَا contributes judicial killing rather than an accidental sinking. For `root_001080/B005`, the sequence “those with him” followed by the definite ٱلْبَاقِينَ visibly partitions the whole field into rescued group and remainder; the bridge from exhaustive partition to this branch makes the verb remove the entire residual group, adding completeness to the drowning.

**Negative evidence.** The tear/saturated-land, bow-drawing, milk-measure, and ornamented-bridle branches have no local trigger. ٱلْمَشْحُونِ supports the ark scene but is not enough by itself to activate those branches.

## 29:40 — فَكُلًّا أَخَذْنَا بِذَنۢبِهِۦ فَمِنْهُم مَّنْ أَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِ حَاصِبًا وَمِنْهُم مَّنْ أَخَذَتْهُ ٱلصَّيْحَةُ وَمِنْهُم مَّنْ خَسَفْنَا بِهِ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَمِنْهُم مَّنْ أَغْرَقْنَا وَمَا كَانَ ٱللَّهُ لِيَظْلِمَهُمْ وَلَٰكِن كَانُوٓا۟ أَنفُسَهُمْ يَظْلِمُونَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** أَغْرَقْنَا states that one of the enumerated groups was drowned as the consequence of its own offense.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Drowning appears as only one member of a rigorously distributed set of punishments: storm of stones, blast, earth-collapse, and drowning are all governed by فَكُلًّا أَخَذْنَا بِذَنۢبِهِۦ. The water event is thereby read through individualized accountability, not through an undifferentiated catastrophe.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) and `root_001080/B005` (entering, surpassing, or taking over a field) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_001080/B002`, أَخَذْنَا بِذَنۢبِهِۦ and the denial of divine ظلم visibly frame every listed event as a punitive taking; the bridge is that drowning functions as a deliberate lethal sentence, sharpening the verb from physical submersion to accountable killing. For `root_001080/B005`, وَمَا كَانُوا۟ سَٰبِقِينَ immediately precedes the list and supplies the branch's explicit “surpassing/outrunning” cue, while فَكُلًّا and repeated وَمِنْهُم map the whole field. The bridge is failed escape from comprehensive seizure: the drowned group cannot outrun the punishment assigned to it, so أَغْرَقْنَا gains a sense of inescapable capture.

**Negative evidence.** No eye/land saturation, milk measure, or decorated bridle is visible. أَرْسَلْنَا عَلَيْهِ حَاصِبًا does not name a bow and is insufficient for the specialized bow-drawing branch.

## 36:43 — وَإِن نَّشَأْ نُغْرِقْهُمْ فَلَا صَرِيخَ لَهُمْ وَلَا هُمْ يُنقَذُونَ

### نُغْرِقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** In the conditional, God can drown the riders; if that occurs, they have neither a responder to their cry nor rescue.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The created means of carriage in 36:41-42 is immediately exposed as contingent rather than self-securing. The exception إِلَّا رَحْمَةً مِّنَّا makes every continued voyage and temporary enjoyment an enacted mercy against the still-possible drowning.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) is retained.

**Activation trace.** فَلَا صَرِيخَ لَهُمْ وَلَا هُمْ يُنقَذُونَ explicitly removes both aid and rescue, and إِلَّا رَحْمَةً supplies the sole contrary outcome. The abductive bridge is that an unrescued causative drowning terminates life, activating `root_001080/B002`; this makes نُغْرِقْ a potential lethal taking whose suspension defines present transport as mercy.

**Negative evidence.** The full ark (ٱلْفُلْكِ ٱلْمَشْحُونِ) is a scene cue, not adequate evidence for saturation, whole-field absorption, bow-drawing, milk, or bridle ornament. No other branch passes the standard.

## 37:82 — ثُمَّ أَغْرَقْنَا ٱلْءَاخَرِينَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** God then drowned the others, in contrast to the faithful servant just commended.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The terse drowning clause is enclosed by moral and genealogical continuity: reward for the doers of good and the believing servant precedes it, while Abraham's affiliation and sound heart follow it. The flood boundary therefore also separates two continuing human alignments.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) is retained.

**Activation trace.** نَجْزِى ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ, عِبَادِنَا ٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ, and the contrasting object ٱلْءَاخَرِينَ visibly divide the rewarded faithful from the drowned others. The bridge is that drowning is the lethal counterpart within this discriminating outcome, so `root_001080/B002` makes أَغْرَقْنَا register as a terminal judgment, not merely a report about water.

**Negative evidence.** ٱلْءَاخَرِينَ identifies the other party but does not by itself establish total absorption under B005. No saturation, bow, milk, or ornament cue appears; no further branch is retained.

## 43:55 — فَلَمَّآ ءَاسَفُونَا ٱنتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ فَأَغْرَقْنَٰهُمْ أَجْمَعِينَ

### أَغْرَقْ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** The verse states that God took vengeance on the people by drowning them all after they provoked wrath.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Their collective obedience to Pharaoh (فَأَطَاعُوهُ) becomes collective destruction (أَجْمَعِينَ), after which the same group is converted into a precedent and example for later people. The social mechanism of following is thus preserved, but reversed into warning.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) and `root_001080/B005` (occupying or absorbing the whole scope) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_001080/B002`, ٱنتَقَمْنَا مِنْهُمْ is the explicit local trigger: the causative drowning is the means of punitive killing, which makes the target verb an execution of vengeance rather than neutral submersion. For `root_001080/B005`, the immediately postposed أَجْمَعِينَ is an unambiguous totality marker. The bridge from “all together” to whole-scope absorption makes the drowning exhaustive, removing the obedient collective as a collective and preparing its conversion into سَلَفًا وَمَثَلًا.

**Negative evidence.** The gold bracelets are ornament, but the specialized B008 branch requires an ornament-covered bridle; neither a bridle nor a covering construction is present. Sorrow/wrath in ءَاسَفُونَا does not establish tear-filled eyes, and the bow and milk branches are untriggered.

## 71:25 — مِّمَّا خَطِيٓـَٰٔتِهِمْ أُغْرِقُوا۟ فَأُدْخِلُوا۟ نَارًا فَلَمْ يَجِدُوا۟ لَهُم مِّن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ أَنصَارًا

### أُغْرِقُ — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** Because of their offenses, the people were drowned, then admitted into fire, and found no helpers apart from God.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The immediate sequence from drowning to entry into fire prevents water from being read as an isolated natural endpoint. It is the first stage of a continuous punitive passage, while the absence of helpers answers the people's earlier insistence on retaining their gods.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B002` (killing by drowning) and `root_001080/B005` (whole-scope absorption) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_001080/B002`, the causal مِّمَّا خَطِيٓـَٰٔتِهِمْ, passive sequence أُغْرِقُوا۟ فَأُدْخِلُوا۟, and absence of أَنصَارًا visibly make the drowning a punitive death leading directly onward; this activates the killing branch and gives the passive verb the force of a sentence carried out. For `root_001080/B005`, the following prayer لَا تَذَرْ ... مِنَ ٱلْكَٰفِرِينَ دَيَّارًا explicitly asks that not one inhabitant remain, with 71:27 explaining the danger of leaving them. The bridge is exhaustive removal, so the drowning is heard as taking the corrupt field without residue.

**Negative evidence.** Entry into fire does not supply a bow, and no tears, saturated land, milk measure, or ornamented bridle is visible. Those branches are not retained.

## 79:1 — وَٱلنَّٰزِعَٰتِ غَرْقًا

### غَرْقًا — غ ر ق

**Primary reading.** غَرْقًا adverbially intensifies ٱلنَّٰزِعَٰتِ: the pull is performed deeply, forcefully, or to the utmost, while the verse leaves the object unstated.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The three clipped parallel clauses move from pulling, to loosening, to swimming, each reinforced by a cognate-style accusative. The local sequence makes the first act one extreme mode within a patterned field of motion rather than a free-standing image.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_001080/B004` (drawing a bow to its limit) and `root_001080/B001` (drowning or sinking in water) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_001080/B004`, the governing participle ٱلنَّٰزِعَٰتِ supplies the exact pulling/drawing action, while غَرْقًا marks its limit. The abductive bridge is the fully tensioned bow-draw: this gives the otherwise abstract intensity a concrete image of extension to the utmost. For `root_001080/B001`, وَٱلسَّٰبِحَٰتِ سَبْحًا two verses later supplies an explicit swimming image within the same parallel sequence. Swimming and drowning are opposed water outcomes; that contrast lets غَرْقًا carry submersive depth alongside force, changing the pull into one that penetrates or engulfs completely without replacing its primary adverbial role.

**Negative evidence.** The sequence does not visibly cue tears or saturated soil, killing by drowning, a milk measure, or an ornamented bridle. General intensity alone is insufficient for B005, so it is not retained.

## 22:19 — هَٰذَانِ خَصْمَانِ ٱخْتَصَمُوا۟ فِى رَبِّهِمْ فَٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ قُطِّعَتْ لَهُمْ ثِيَابٌ مِّن نَّارٍ يُصَبُّ مِن فَوْقِ رُءُوسِهِمُ ٱلْحَمِيمُ

### حَمِيمُ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** ٱلْحَمِيمُ is scalding water poured from above the unbelievers' heads, paired with garments cut from fire.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding universal prostration and promised separation on the Day of Resurrection narrow into a bodily scene: a cosmic dispute is resolved upon heads, bellies, and skins. The hot water carries judgment from the level of groups into and across the body.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000001/B003` (rendering fat or suet) and `root_000001/B006` (a matter becoming due, present, or binding) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000001/B003`, يُصْهَرُ بِهِۦ with بُطُونِهِمْ and ٱلْجُلُودُ is a visible rendering/melting process applied to bodies. The bridge is that the hot liquid performs on bodily material what heat does in rendering, so ٱلْحَمِيمُ becomes an active solvent of interior and exterior rather than merely a painful pour. For `root_000001/B006`, وَكَثِيرٌ حَقَّ عَلَيْهِ ٱلْعَذَابُ states immediately beforehand that punishment has become binding/due, and يَوْمَ ٱلْقِيَٰمَةِ supplies its appointed arrival. This activates the due/imminent branch and makes the poured water the concrete arrival of an obligation already fixed upon its recipients.

**Negative evidence.** Fire and melting do not explicitly supply charcoal, soot, sweat, fever, or a bathhouse vessel. No horse sound, deliberate pursuit, intimate kin, bird, or choice-property cue appears. The fate branch B010 overlaps conceptually with due punishment, but the wording حَقَّ عَلَيْهِ more exactly supports B006, so B010 is not separately retained.

## 37:67 — ثُمَّ إِنَّ لَهُمْ عَلَيْهَا لَشَوْبًا مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ

### حَمِيمٍ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** After eating and filling their bellies from the tree, they receive an admixture of scalding water upon it.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The water is not presented as relief after eating but as شَوْبًا, an added mixture that completes the meal before the eaters are returned to the blaze. Drinking is folded into the same coercive consumption as eating.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000001/B011` (near kin or intimate companion) is retained.

**Activation trace.** The closing verse إِنَّهُمْ أَلْفَوْا۟ ءَابَآءَهُمْ ضَآلِّينَ gives an explicit near-kin trigger, ءَابَآءَهُمْ, and makes following those fathers the local explanation for the depicted destination. The abductive bridge is ironic companionship: the ancestral closeness they followed accompanies them into the same return, so `root_000001/B011` makes حَمِيمٍ resonate not only as their drink but as the bitter “intimate” attached to inherited error.

**Negative evidence.** Food, mixture, and filled bellies do not identify rendered fat, and ٱلْجَحِيمِ alone is too broad to establish the separate sweat/summer-heat or fever branches. No blackness, due-time, horse sound, pursuit, bird, bathhouse, or property cue passes the standard.

## 38:57 — هَٰذَا فَلْيَذُوقُوهُ حَمِيمٌ وَغَسَّاقٌ

### حَمِيمٌ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The command makes the inhabitants taste scalding water together with غَسَّاقٌ as named punishments.

**Ayah-level surprise.** حَمِيمٌ is placed in an open-ended taxonomy: it is paired with غَسَّاقٌ, followed by “others of its kind, in pairs,” and then encountered by another entering company. The liquid is one repeatable member of an ordered infernal assortment, not a solitary image.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000001/B001` (blackness, charcoal, or dark smoke) and `root_000001/B004` (intense heat and its effects) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000001/B001`, the direct coordination وَغَسَّاقٌ supplies a darkening root cue (غ س ق), and مِن شَكْلِهِۦٓ أَزْوَٰجٌ explicitly groups similar paired kinds. The bridge is chromatic association within the pair: the scalding liquid is heard as belonging to a dark, smoke-blackened register as well as being hot. For `root_000001/B004`, جَهَنَّمَ يَصْلَوْنَهَا before the focus and صَالُوا۟ ٱلنَّارِ after it visibly enclose the drink in sustained fire exposure. This turns the heat of حَمِيمٌ from a property of one liquid into part of an all-surrounding thermal punishment.

**Negative evidence.** Tasting does not establish rendered fat or fever, and the entering فوج “with you” denotes shared location rather than intimate kinship. No due-time, horse sound, pursuit, bird, bathhouse vessel, or choice-property trigger appears.
