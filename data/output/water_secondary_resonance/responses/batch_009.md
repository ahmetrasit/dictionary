# batch_009 Water Secondary Resonance

## 24:40 — أَوْ كَظُلُمَٰتٍ فِى بَحْرٍ لُّجِّىٍّ يَغْشَىٰهُ مَوْجٌ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ سَحَابٌ ظُلُمَٰتٌۢ بَعْضُهَا فَوْقَ بَعْضٍ إِذَآ أَخْرَجَ يَدَهُۥ لَمْ يَكَدْ يَرَىٰهَا وَمَن لَّمْ يَجْعَلِ ٱللَّهُ لَهُۥ نُورًا فَمَا لَهُۥ مِن نُّورٍ

### بَحْرٍ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The noun supplies the actual deep sea in which the comparison's darkness, waves, and loss of sight occur.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The preceding ayah's thirsty person mistakes a mirage for water and finds nothing; the focus ayah answers absence with overwhelming real water, yet both scenes terminate in failed perception.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth) and `root_000086/B014` (severe unquenched thirst) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000086/B002`, the local stack `بَحْرٍ لُّجِّىٍّ ... مَوْجٌ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌ ... سَحَابٌ` supplies depth and repeated vertical extension; breadth therefore acts as the spatial amplifier that turns the sea into an encompassing field of occlusion. For `root_000086/B014`, the immediately preceding `ٱلظَّمْـَٔانُ` who approaches supposed `مَآءً` but `لَمْ يَجِدْهُ شَيْـًٔا` is an unusually explicit trigger for thirst left unquenched; the sea consequently carries an irony of abundance that still cannot give the seeker access or relief.

**Negative evidence.** The darkness and inability to see do not by themselves establish `root_000086/B008` bewilderment, and no local form supports the cleft, salt, travel, disease, or later medical branches.

### مَوْجٌ — م و ج (first occurrence, 24:40:7)

**Primary reading.** The first wave rises over and covers the person or deep-sea scene.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its role is not merely maritime motion: the following second wave and cloud recruit it into a layered architecture of darkness.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `يَغْشَىٰهُ` and the sequence `مَوْجٌ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌ` strongly intensify the accepted turbulence image, but the inventory has no other `م و ج` branch to activate; the cues therefore refine the primary sense only.

**Negative evidence.** No branch ID beyond `root_001455/B001` exists in the supplied target inventory, so the repetition cannot license an invented secondary branch.

### مَوْجٌ — م و ج (second occurrence, 24:40:10)

**Primary reading.** The second wave is the upper wave, placed above the first and below the cloud.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Repetition converts an individual wave into a recursive layer: water, wave, wave, and cloud make physical turbulence a model for compounded obscurity.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The construction `مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ مَوْجٌ مِّن فَوْقِهِۦ سَحَابٌ` gives the second occurrence its distinct stacking role, but this remains within `root_001455/B001` rather than activating another accepted branch.

**Negative evidence.** No additional `م و ج` branch is present in the inventory; neither darkness nor cloud supplies one.

## 25:53 — وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى مَرَجَ ٱلْبَحْرَيْنِ هَٰذَا عَذْبٌ فُرَاتٌ وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌ وَجَعَلَ بَيْنَهُمَا بَرْزَخًا وَحِجْرًا مَّحْجُورًا

### بَحْرَيْنِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The dual denotes the two bodies of water, one fresh and one intensely salty, brought into relation while a boundary remains between them.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The next ayah's `خَلَقَ مِنَ ٱلْمَآءِ بَشَرًا` and `نَسَبًا وَصِهْرًا` places the bounded relation of waters beside the making of human relational bonds: contact does not erase distinction.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B004` (saltiness) is retained for the salty member of the dual.

**Activation trace.** The explicit predication `وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌ` is the visible trigger for `root_000086/B004`; saltiness differentiates one referent inside `بَحْرَيْنِ`, so the dual is heard not as two interchangeable expanses but as a paired unity with opposed water qualities.

**Negative evidence.** `بَرْزَخًا وَحِجْرًا مَّحْجُورًا` marks a barrier, not the cutting action required for `root_000086/B003`; no adequate cue activates breadth, travel, encounter, thirst, or the high-burden peripheral branches.

### فُرَاتٌ — ف ر ت

**Primary reading.** The adjective identifies the first water as fresh and sweet.

**Ayah-level surprise.** `مَرَجَ` places that sweetness in contact with its saline opposite, while `بَرْزَخًا` and `حِجْرًا مَّحْجُورًا` show its identity maintained rather than dissolved.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The deictic contrast `هَٰذَا ... وَهَٰذَا` and the boundary sharpen the primary fresh-water quality, but nothing names the proper river or a decline in intellect.

**Negative evidence.** Neither `root_001137/B002` nor `root_001137/B003` has a local trigger; the window supplies no river name and no cognitive weakening.

### أُجَاجٌ — ء ج ج

**Primary reading.** The adjective presents the second water as bitterly, intensely salty.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its harsh quality remains one element of a deliberately held contrast: the two seas are set in relation, yet the repeated demonstratives preserve their different identities.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `مِلْحٌ` directly fixes the target in the accepted saline sense; `مَرَجَ` adds movement or contact at ayah level but supplies no audible rustling, blaze, or heat that would move the target into another `ء ج ج` branch.

**Negative evidence.** `root_000014/B001`, `root_000014/B002`, and `root_000014/B004` are not retained: agitation is not audibly marked, heat is absent, and generic intensity would merely restate the primary salt sense.

## 26:63 — فَأَوْحَيْنَآ إِلَىٰ مُوسَىٰٓ أَنِ ٱضْرِب بِّعَصَاكَ ٱلْبَحْرَ فَٱنفَلَقَ فَكَانَ كُلُّ فِرْقٍ كَٱلطَّوْدِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ

### بَحْرَ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The accusative noun is the sea struck by Moses' staff.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The pursuers' apparent closure in `إِنَّا لَمُدْرَكُونَ` is reversed when the obstructing sea itself becomes separated towering sides and a route of rescue.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B003` (wide cleft) is retained.

**Activation trace.** The sequence `ٱضْرِب ... ٱلْبَحْرَ فَٱنفَلَقَ فَكَانَ كُلُّ فِرْقٍ` visibly supplies strike, splitting, and separated parts; `root_000086/B003` acts as the opening image, changing `ٱلْبَحْرَ` from a body of water acted upon into the very medium cut open for passage.

**Negative evidence.** `كَٱلطَّوْدِ ٱلْعَظِيمِ` magnifies the resulting parts but does not independently activate the general breadth branch, and no cue supports saltiness, travel, bewilderment, or the peripheral senses.

## 27:61 — أَمَّن جَعَلَ ٱلْأَرْضَ قَرَارًا وَجَعَلَ خِلَٰلَهَآ أَنْهَٰرًا وَجَعَلَ لَهَا رَوَٰسِىَ وَجَعَلَ بَيْنَ ٱلْبَحْرَيْنِ حَاجِزًا أَءِلَٰهٌ مَّعَ ٱللَّهِ بَلْ أَكْثَرُهُمْ لَا يَعْلَمُونَ

### بَحْرَيْنِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The dual names two bodies of water between which a barrier has been placed.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sea boundary appears inside a repeated sequence of terrestrial stabilizations: earth as `قَرَارًا`, rivers through it, firm mountains, and finally a barrier between waters.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `حَاجِزًا` gives the two seas a relational limit and the surrounding `أَنْهَٰرًا` broadens the hydrological scene, but neither cue matches another accepted `ب ح ر` branch closely enough to alter the target word itself.

**Negative evidence.** A barrier between seas is not a slit or cutting event for `root_000086/B003`; saltiness is not stated, and breadth, travel, exposure, and the high-burden branches lack specific triggers.

## 27:63 — أَمَّن يَهْدِيكُمْ فِى ظُلُمَٰتِ ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ وَمَن يُرْسِلُ ٱلرِّيَٰحَ بُشْرًۢا بَيْنَ يَدَىْ رَحْمَتِهِۦٓ أَءِلَٰهٌ مَّعَ ٱللَّهِ تَعَٰلَى ٱللَّهُ عَمَّا يُشْرِكُونَ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the sea as one of the two domains, with land, whose darknesses require guidance.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sea is framed not only by darkness but by directed movement: guidance through it and winds sent ahead of mercy turn an obscuring domain into a traversable one.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained.

**Activation trace.** `يَهْدِيكُمْ فِى ظُلُمَٰتِ ... ٱلْبَحْرِ` provides travel requiring direction, and `يُرْسِلُ ٱلرِّيَٰحَ` supplies its maritime enabling image; `root_000086/B009` therefore makes the sea a route actually crossed under guidance, not merely a water body listed beside land.

**Negative evidence.** Darkness alone does not establish `root_000086/B008` bewilderment, and no local cue supports cleaving, saltiness, unplanned encounter, thirst, or disease.

## 30:41 — ظَهَرَ ٱلْفَسَادُ فِى ٱلْبَرِّ وَٱلْبَحْرِ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِى ٱلنَّاسِ لِيُذِيقَهُم بَعْضَ ٱلَّذِى عَمِلُوا۟ لَعَلَّهُمْ يَرْجِعُونَ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is one of the two comprehensive earthly domains, alongside land, in which corruption has appeared through human action.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sequence moves from human acquisition (`كَسَبَتْ أَيْدِى ٱلنَّاسِ`) to experienced consequence (`لِيُذِيقَهُم`) and possible return, so the environment becomes a disclosed register of action.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B005` (open exposure) is retained, with its formulaic scope kept narrow.

**Activation trace.** The ayah opens with the exact manifestation cue `ظَهَرَ` and locates what has become manifest `فِى ... ٱلْبَحْرِ`; this unusually visible trigger supplies the open/exposed role of `root_000086/B005`. The bridge is not that sea means exposure ordinarily, but that this sea is the arena in which concealed consequences come into view, changing it from a passive location into a field of disclosure.

**Negative evidence.** The land-sea pairing alone is too broad to activate `root_000086/B002`, while `ٱلْفَسَادُ` and `لِيُذِيقَهُم` do not specifically establish wasting disease or unquenched thirst; no other branch is retained.

## 31:27 — وَلَوْ أَنَّمَا فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ مِن شَجَرَةٍ أَقْلَٰمٌ وَٱلْبَحْرُ يَمُدُّهُۥ مِنۢ بَعْدِهِۦ سَبْعَةُ أَبْحُرٍ مَّا نَفِدَتْ كَلِمَٰتُ ٱللَّهِ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ عَزِيزٌ حَكِيمٌ

### بَحْرُ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The singular sea supplies the imagined ink source extended by seven more seas.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Trees become pens and seas become replenishing ink, yet even this enormous conversion of earth's materials remains finite before `كَلِمَٰتُ ٱللَّهِ`.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth) is retained.

**Activation trace.** `يَمُدُّهُۥ` makes the sea an extendable quantity, `سَبْعَةُ أَبْحُرٍ` multiplies it, and `مَا نَفِدَتْ` measures that abundance against inexhaustible words; `root_000086/B002` thus turns the singular sea into a deliberately maximal measure of material vastness whose insufficiency sharpens the comparison.

**Negative evidence.** Nothing in the writing-and-extension construction triggers cleaving, saltiness, sea travel, exposure, thirst, disease, or the other peripheral branches.

### أَبْحُرٍ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The plural denotes seven additional seas imagined as replenishing the first sea's ink.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Multiplication does not approach adequacy: even the pluralized reserve is placed under the negated exhaustion of God's words.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B002` (expanse and breadth) is retained.

**Activation trace.** The numeral `سَبْعَةُ`, the support relation `مِنۢ بَعْدِهِۦ`, and the result `مَا نَفِدَتْ` make breadth an explicit quantitative role; `root_000086/B002` changes `أَبْحُرٍ` from a mere count of water bodies into successive layers of vast provision that still fail as a measure.

**Negative evidence.** The plural form itself does not license travel, salt, cleaving, or relation branches, and the window contains no specific cue for any high-burden sense.

## 31:31 — أَلَمْ تَرَ أَنَّ ٱلْفُلْكَ تَجْرِى فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ بِنِعْمَتِ ٱللَّهِ لِيُرِيَكُم مِّنْ ءَايَٰتِهِۦٓ إِنَّ فِى ذَٰلِكَ لَءَايَٰتٍ لِّكُلِّ صَبَّارٍ شَكُورٍ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The sea is the water body through which the ships run.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The next ayah exposes the same passage as crisis: a wave like canopies prompts exclusive prayer, and rescue to land tests whether seeing the sign becomes acknowledgment.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained.

**Activation trace.** The concrete construction `ٱلْفُلْكَ تَجْرِى فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ` directly depicts sea travel, while `غَشِيَهُم مَّوْجٌ` and `نَجَّىٰهُمْ إِلَى ٱلْبَرِّ` trace its danger and endpoint; `root_000086/B009` therefore changes the sea from static setting into a journey whose movement, peril, and deliverance disclose signs.

**Negative evidence.** The covering wave may imply fear but does not explicitly establish `root_000086/B008` bewilderment; no cleft, salt, exposure, or thirst branch receives a sufficient cue.

## 35:12 — وَمَا يَسْتَوِى ٱلْبَحْرَانِ هَٰذَا عَذْبٌ فُرَاتٌ سَآئِغٌ شَرَابُهُۥ وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌ وَمِن كُلٍّ تَأْكُلُونَ لَحْمًا طَرِيًّا وَتَسْتَخْرِجُونَ حِلْيَةً تَلْبَسُونَهَا وَتَرَى ٱلْفُلْكَ فِيهِ مَوَاخِرَ لِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ وَلَعَلَّكُمْ تَشْكُرُونَ

### بَحْرَانِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The dual names two unequal water bodies, one sweet with an easy drink and the other intensely salty.

**Ayah-level surprise.** `وَمَا يَسْتَوِى` establishes qualitative inequality, but `وَمِن كُلٍّ` immediately gives both a shared economy of food, adornment, travel, bounty, and gratitude.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B004` (saltiness) and `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) are retained.

**Activation trace.** For `root_000086/B004`, `وَهَٰذَا مِلْحٌ أُجَاجٌ` explicitly marks the saline member, making `ٱلْبَحْرَانِ` an internally contrasted dual rather than a homogeneous pair. For `root_000086/B009`, `وَتَرَى ٱلْفُلْكَ فِيهِ مَوَاخِرَ لِتَبْتَغُوا۟ مِن فَضْلِهِۦ` visibly supplies ships moving through the water for gain; the dual seas consequently function not only as substances of different taste but as navigated fields of provision.

**Negative evidence.** The contrast does not supply a cleaving event, and neither shared produce nor the fresh drink specifically activates breadth, exposure, thirst, disease, or the other peripheral branches.

### فُرَاتٌ — ف ر ت

**Primary reading.** The adjective gives the first sea its fresh, sweet water quality.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its sweetness is made bodily accessible by `سَآئِغٌ شَرَابُهُۥ`, yet the following `وَمِن كُلٍّ` prevents drinkability from becoming the only measure of a sea's benefit.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `عَذْبٌ` and `سَآئِغٌ شَرَابُهُۥ` mutually sharpen the ordinary fresh-water sense; no construction points to the named river or to weakening after mental steadiness.

**Negative evidence.** `root_001137/B002` and `root_001137/B003` lack visible local triggers, so neither is retained.

### شَرَابُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun is the drink furnished by the fresh sea, characterized as easy to swallow.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink appears inside a larger catalogue of provision from both unequal seas, so immediate ingestion is one mode of benefit among food, adornment, navigation, and sought bounty.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The possessive `شَرَابُهُۥ` refers back to `هَٰذَا عَذْبٌ فُرَاتٌ`, and `سَآئِغٌ` fixes literal drinkability; the nearby salty contrast does not transfer an unpleasant-water sense onto this explicitly agreeable drink.

**Negative evidence.** `root_000783/B005` is contradicted rather than activated by `سَآئِغٌ`; no allotted share, drinking group, vessel/place, permeation, understanding, or other accepted branch has a specific cue.

### أُجَاجٌ — ء ج ج

**Primary reading.** The adjective marks the second sea as bitterly, intensely salty.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Although it cannot equal the fresh sea in taste, `وَمِن كُلٍّ` includes it in the same productive sequence, so harshness does not entail uselessness.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `مِلْحٌ` anchors the saline branch and the fresh sea's `سَآئِغٌ شَرَابُهُۥ` supplies a precise contrast; the ships `مَوَاخِرَ` add motion to the scene but do not visibly supply the sound or agitation required for another `ء ج ج` branch.

**Negative evidence.** The earlier `عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ` is too remote and generic to activate `root_000014/B004`, while no fire, heat, blaze, or audible rustling supports `root_000014/B001` or `root_000014/B002`.

## 42:32 — وَمِنْ ءَايَٰتِهِ ٱلْجَوَارِ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ كَٱلْأَعْلَٰمِ

### بَحْرِ — ب ح ر

**Primary reading.** The noun is the sea in which the sailing vessels move, appearing like great landmarks or mountains.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The following alternatives make the sign depend on controlled motion: the wind may be stilled so the ships remain motionless, or the ships may be destroyed because of what people earned.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000086/B009` (sea travel and relation) is retained.

**Activation trace.** `ٱلْجَوَارِ فِى ٱلْبَحْرِ` is a direct travel image, while `يُسْكِنِ ٱلرِّيحَ فَيَظْلَلْنَ رَوَاكِدَ` makes their ability not to travel equally visible; `root_000086/B009` therefore turns the sea into a contingent route whose movement depends on wind and whose travelers remain exposed to stoppage or destruction.

**Negative evidence.** `كَٱلْأَعْلَٰمِ` expresses the ships' scale, not the sea's lexical breadth, and the danger in `يُوبِقْهُنَّ` does not specifically trigger bewilderment, disease, thirst, or another `ب ح ر` branch.
