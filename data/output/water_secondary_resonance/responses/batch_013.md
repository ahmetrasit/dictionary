# batch_013 Water Secondary Resonance

## 76:6 — عَيْنًا يَشْرَبُ بِهَا عِبَادُ ٱللَّهِ يُفَجِّرُونَهَا تَفْجِيرًا

### يَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The verb contributes literal drinking: God's servants drink from a spring which they cause to flow abundantly.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The spring stands between the chains, shackles, and blaze prepared for rejecters (76:4) and the servants' fulfillment, fear, and feeding of the needy, orphan, and captive (76:7-8). Their reception of freely flowing drink is thus paired with their own giving rather than mere consumption.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B008` (putting a rope around the neck) is activated contrastively: drinking at the spring becomes bodily freedom opposite the neck-restraint image in the window. `root_000783/B007` (permeating or being imbued) is also retained: the drink can echo an inward saturation by the `حُبّ` that accompanies their feeding, without displacing the literal drink.

**Activation trace.** For B008, `سَلَاسِلَا وَأَغْلَالًا` in 76:4 supplies an unusually exact restraint trigger; the bridge is the contrast between forced binding at the neck and servants approaching and channeling their drink, which makes `يَشْرَبُ` a sign of uncoerced access. For B007, `عَلَىٰ حُبِّهِ` in 76:8 supplies the heart-affection cue named in that branch; the bridge is that what enters as drink corresponds to an affection that has entered deeply enough to issue in feeding others, adding inward formation to bodily refreshment.

**Negative evidence.** The plural drinkers in 76:5 and the collective `عِبَادُ ٱللَّهِ` do not by themselves establish B003; they remain ordinary participants in literal drinking. No allotment, vessel, unpleasant-water, understanding, or technical waterskin cue supports the other branches.

## 76:21 — عَٰلِيَهُمْ ثِيَابُ سُندُسٍ خُضْرٌ وَإِسْتَبْرَقٌ وَحُلُّوٓا۟ أَسَاوِرَ مِن فِضَّةٍ وَسَقَىٰهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ شَرَابًا طَهُورًا

### سَقَىٰ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb presents their Lord as the direct giver of a purifying drink.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The ayah moves from visible clothing and silver adornment to a drink given by the Lord, then identifies the whole scene as recompense for acknowledged effort (76:22). Exterior honor is completed by an interior gift.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B009` (soaking cloth with dye) is retained. It makes the divine giving of drink echo the saturation of the richly colored clothing immediately before it, so the drink reads as an inward counterpart to the wearers' outward coloration.

**Activation trace.** `ثِيَابُ سُندُسٍ خُضْرٌ وَإِسْتَبْرَقٌ` supplies both the cloth and color cues required by B009. The bridge is the ayah's direct sequence from colored garments to `سَقَىٰهُمْ`; the branch contributes an image of saturation, and `طَهُورًا` directs that image toward permeating purity rather than merely quenching thirst.

**Negative evidence.** `جَزَاءً` establishes reward but not specifically a water-share, so B003 is not retained. There is no rain-prayer, cloud, belly-fluid, or forced-heart cue for B005-B007 or B010.

### شَرَابًا — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the actual drink given by their Lord, expressly characterized as purifying.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink is the inward member of a sequence otherwise dominated by things seen and worn: green silk, brocade, and silver bracelets. The next ayah makes this embodied transformation a recompense for their striving.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B007` (permeating or being imbued) is retained. The drink is not recast as metaphorical; rather, its literal ingestion acquires the additional suggestion that purity permeates the drinkers as color permeates their garments.

**Activation trace.** The immediate `ثِيَابُ ... خُضْرٌ` provides B007's visible color-suffusion image, while `طَهُورًا` gives the quality proposed to permeate. The bridge is the outer-to-inner sequence in one ayah; this changes `شَرَابًا` from an item of refreshment alone into the medium of an inwardly absorbed purity.

**Negative evidence.** Neither the nearby reward language nor the attendants establishes an allotted water-turn or a company of co-drinkers. No vessel, unpleasant-water, neck, understanding, or waterskin trigger is visible.

## 77:43 — كُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟ هَنِيٓـًٔۢا بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

### ٱشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The imperative grants the addressees literal, wholesome enjoyment of drink alongside food.

**Ayah-level surprise.** What they desire in the preceding fruit scene is immediately tied to what they used to do, and the next ayah names this as recompense for those who do good. Appetite is not detached indulgence here but enjoyment proportioned to action, against the following warning to deniers.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B002` (an allotted share of water) is retained. It adds to the imperative the sense of an assigned, fitting portion within recompense, while the commanded act remains literal drinking.

**Activation trace.** `بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ` and `كَذَٰلِكَ نَجْزِى ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَ` visibly construe the food and drink as a consequence and award. The abductive bridge is from recompense proportioned to deeds to B002's allotted portion; this makes `ٱشْرَبُوا` reception of one's granted share, not merely permission to consume.

**Negative evidence.** `هَنِيئًا` confirms pleasant literal drinking and therefore does not activate the unpleasant-water branch B005. The collective imperative alone is insufficient for B003, and no vessel, color-permeation, restraint, or understanding cue appears.

## 78:24 — لَّا يَذُوقُونَ فِيهَا بَرْدًا وَلَا شَرَابًا

### شَرَابًا — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes drink as a denied form of relief: the inhabitants taste neither coolness nor drink there, apart from the named exceptions in 78:25.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Prolonged residence (78:23) does not normalize the place into habitation; even the paired basics of coolness and drink are negated. The exception `حَمِيمًا وَغَسَّاقًا`, followed by `جَزَاءً وِفَاقًا`, turns what should relieve into an exact mode of recompense.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained. The branch sharpens the exception: “drink” survives only at the threshold of something ingestible while its ordinary promise of refreshment is stripped away.

**Activation trace.** The construction `لَا ... شَرَابًا إِلَّا حَمِيمًا وَغَسَّاقًا` is the local trigger: categorical denial is followed by two explicitly named substitutes, one directly paired with the denied drink. The bridge to B005 is necessity-level or aversive drinkability; this changes `شَرَابًا` into a bitter category boundary rather than a benefit.

**Negative evidence.** The window has no share, drinking company, vessel, color, restraint, understanding, or vegetation cue. The duration and recompense language affect the ayah-level force but do not activate another branch of ش ر ب.

## 83:28 — عَيْنًا يَشْرَبُ بِهَا ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ

### يَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The verb contributes literal drinking by those brought near from the spring associated with the preceding mixture of Tasnim.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The invitation to compete in the sealed, musk-finished drink (83:26) resolves not in possession of an object but in proximity to a source. The following mockery of believers (83:29-30) makes that true nearness answer a social order that had treated them as laughable.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B004` (drinking place or source) is retained. It makes the act of drinking carry locative force: the privilege of the brought-near is direct relation to the spring, not only receipt of its liquid.

**Activation trace.** `عَيْنًا` and the construction `يَشْرَبُ بِهَا` visibly present a source with which the drinking is performed, while `ٱلْمُقَرَّبُونَ` emphasizes proximity. The bridge is from proximity to a named spring to B004's drinking-place/source branch; the added contribution is access at the source itself.

**Negative evidence.** `فَلْيَتَنَافَسِ` can suggest aspiration but does not establish an allotted water-turn, so B002 is not retained. The plural subject is not enough for B003, and no unpleasantness, permeation, restraint, or understanding cue appears.

## 2:71 — قَالَ إِنَّهُۥ يَقُولُ إِنَّهَا بَقَرَةٌ لَّا ذَلُولٌ تُثِيرُ ٱلْأَرْضَ وَلَا تَسْقِى ٱلْحَرْثَ مُسَلَّمَةٌ لَّا شِيَةَ فِيهَا قَالُوا۟ ٱلْـَٰٔنَ جِئْتَ بِٱلْحَقِّ فَذَبَحُوهَا وَمَا كَادُوا۟ يَفْعَلُونَ

### تَسْقِى — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** In the negated clause, the verb describes agricultural watering: the specified cow has not been used to irrigate cultivated land, just as it has not been trained to stir the earth.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The questioners' repeated concern with identifying appearance in 2:69-70 culminates in a description joining labor history to an unmarked body. Only after color, work, and marking have all been specified do they claim that the truth has arrived, yet they nearly fail to act.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B009` (soaking cloth with dye) is retained contrastively. The negated irrigation sits inside a window saturated with color language, so `تَسْقِى` participates secondarily in an image of something neither worked by water nor marked by color.

**Activation trace.** `لَوْنُهَا`, `صَفْرَاءُ فَاقِعٌ لَّوْنُهَا` (2:69), and `لَا شِيَةَ فِيهَا` (2:71) provide repeated visible color and marking triggers for B009. The bridge is the shared saturation pattern between watering and dye-soaking; because both irrigation and marking are negated or excluded around the cow, the target additionally helps portray an untouched specimen rather than only an animal lacking one farm task.

**Negative evidence.** B003's irrigation share/channel domain belongs to the ordinary agricultural reading here and is not counted as a secondary resonance. No belly-fluid, rain-prayer, rain-cloud, reed, or forced-heart cue is visible.

## 9:19 — أَجَعَلْتُمْ سِقَايَةَ ٱلْحَآجِّ وَعِمَارَةَ ٱلْمَسْجِدِ ٱلْحَرَامِ كَمَنْ ءَامَنَ بِٱللَّهِ وَٱلْيَوْمِ ٱلْءَاخِرِ وَجَٰهَدَ فِى سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ لَا يَسْتَوُۥنَ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ وَٱللَّهُ لَا يَهْدِى ٱلْقَوْمَ ٱلظَّٰلِمِينَ

### سِقَايَةَ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes the service of giving drink to pilgrims, placed alongside maintenance of the Sacred Mosque.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window first contrasts two kinds of mosque association (9:17-18), then refuses to equate conspicuous sanctuary service with faith and striving. The following ayat relocate greatness and success from public function to committed expenditure of wealth and self.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `سِقَايَةَ ٱلْحَاجِّ` supplies a recipient and therefore securely establishes the ordinary giving-drink service, but no further local form or image bridges it to another listed branch.

**Negative evidence.** The religious setting does not itself activate B006's specific prayer for water or rain. Mosque maintenance is not an irrigation channel, and the window contains no belly fluid, rain cloud, water-fed reed, dyed cloth, or forced hostility entering a heart.

## 12:41 — يَٰصَىٰحِبَىِ ٱلسِّجْنِ أَمَّآ أَحَدُكُمَا فَيَسْقِى رَبَّهُۥ خَمْرًا وَأَمَّا ٱلْءَاخَرُ فَيُصْلَبُ فَتَأْكُلُ ٱلطَّيْرُ مِن رَّأْسِهِۦ قُضِىَ ٱلْأَمْرُ ٱلَّذِى فِيهِ تَسْتَفْتِيَانِ

### يَسْقِى — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb means that one prisoner will give or serve wine to his master.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A routine act of service is made the compact sign of one prisoner's survival and restored station, while the parallel future of the other is execution and bodily consumption. The following failed remembrance extends the contrast: the restored servant can carry wine yet does not immediately carry Joseph's request into speech.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** The explicit object `خَمْرًا` and recipient `رَبَّهُ` fully account for B001's ordinary giving-drink sense. The parallel execution, remembrance, and forgetting alter the narrative weight of the act but provide no lexical bridge to a different س ق ي branch.

**Negative evidence.** The grim neighboring outcome is insufficient for B010: no heart, repeated hostile words, or forced inward dislike appears. There is likewise no enduring water source, irrigation, belly fluid, rain prayer, cloud, reed, or dye-soaking cue.

## 12:70 — فَلَمَّا جَهَّزَهُم بِجَهَازِهِمْ جَعَلَ ٱلسِّقَايَةَ فِى رَحْلِ أَخِيهِ ثُمَّ أَذَّنَ مُؤَذِّنٌ أَيَّتُهَا ٱلْعِيرُ إِنَّكُمْ لَسَٰرِقُونَ

### سِّقَايَةَ — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** Here the noun is a drinking vessel placed in the brother's baggage; 12:72 identifies the missing object as the king's `صُوَاع`.

**Ayah-level surprise.** An object ordinarily associated with drinking is concealed as the mechanism of a public theft accusation. The private disclosure of brotherhood in 12:69 is thus followed by a public cry that temporarily reverses protection into apparent incrimination.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `جَعَلَ ٱلسِّقَايَةَ فِى رَحْلِ أَخِيهِ`, followed by `نَفْقِدُ صُوَاعَ ٱلْمَلِكِ`, visibly fixes the target as a portable vessel. That supports the primary reading only; it does not provide a warranted bridge to any different branch present in this packet's inventory.

**Negative evidence.** The supplied س ق ي inventory has no vessel branch, and B001 and B002 explicitly exclude the vessel called `سقاء` or `سقاية`; assigning either would violate the branch boundaries. The accusation does not by itself establish B010's repeated hostility forced into a heart, and no irrigation, belly-fluid, rain, reed, or dye cue appears.

## 23:21 — وَإِنَّ لَكُمْ فِى ٱلْأَنْعَٰمِ لَعِبْرَةً نُّسْقِيكُم مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهَا وَلَكُمْ فِيهَا مَنَٰفِعُ كَثِيرَةٌ وَمِنْهَا تَأْكُلُونَ

### نُّسْقِي — س ق ي

**Primary reading.** The verb presents the addressees as being given drink from what is inside the cattle's bellies, one benefit among many that also include food.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The window moves through gardens and fruit, a tree yielding oil and `صِبْغ` for eaters, drink from inside cattle, and carriage on animals and ships. Provision repeatedly arrives by converting a concealed or rooted source into something bodily useful.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000722/B005` (fluid gathered in the belly) and `root_000722/B009` (soaking cloth with dye) are retained. B005 foregrounds the unlikely bodily enclosure from which the drink is supplied; B009 places that supply beside the preceding image of a coloring or saturating substance, adding material absorption to the sequence of benefits.

**Activation trace.** For B005, `مِّمَّا فِى بُطُونِهَا` is an exact locative trigger for enclosed bodily fluid. The bridge does not make the beneficial drink pathological; it uses the branch's enclosure image to emphasize transformation from hidden belly-fluid to potable provision. For B009, `وَصِبْغٍ لِّلْآكِلِينَ` in 23:20 is the branch's explicit dye/color cue; the immediate sequence from `صِبْغ` to `نُّسْقِيكُم` links two forms of material saturation, making the drink one instance in a broader provision of substances that can enter and benefit bodies.

**Negative evidence.** The gardens, palms, grapes, and growing tree make irrigation imaginable but do not mention a land-share or channel, so B003 is not retained. There is no prayer for rain, rain-heavy cloud, water-fed reed, or repeated hostility entering a heart.
