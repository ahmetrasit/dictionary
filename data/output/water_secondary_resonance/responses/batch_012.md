# batch_012 Water Secondary Resonance

## 26:155 — قَالَ هَٰذِهِۦ نَاقَةٌ لَّهَا شِرْبٌ وَلَكُمْ شِرْبُ يَوْمٍ مَّعْلُومٍ

### شِرْبٌ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun assigns the she-camel her allotted turn or share of water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The requested sign arrives not merely as a spectacle but as a regulated claim on a shared resource; the next two ayat make violation of that arrangement the concrete form of rejecting the sign.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers) is retained. It gives the she-camel's share a relational dimension: she is treated as a participant in a watering community rather than as an object that happens to consume water.

**Activation trace.** The reciprocal construction `لَّهَا شِرْبٌ وَلَكُمْ شِرْبُ` visibly sets the camel (`نَاقَةٌ`, root ن و ق) against the human plural `لَكُمْ`, while `يَوْمٍ مَّعْلُومٍ` fixes their turns. B003 supplies the category of watering participants; the bridge is that reciprocal allocation presupposes co-users of one water regime. The target consequently marks recognized participant-status as well as quantity.

**Negative evidence.** No place, vessel, unpleasant-water quality, permeation, rope, neck-extension, false charge, understanding, expenditure, waterskin, or watered-land cue is present; those branches are not retained.

### شِرْبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun assigns the addressed people their known day's allotted turn or share of water.

**Ayah-level surprise.** A temporal entitlement becomes an ethical test: the following prohibition and their hamstringing of the camel show that honoring the sign means accepting a limit on communal access.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B003` (drinkers and co-drinkers) is retained. It makes the people's share define them as one party within a reciprocal watering relationship, not as unrestricted owners of the supply.

**Activation trace.** Plural `لَكُمْ`, paired with singular `لَّهَا`, and the scheduling phrase `يَوْمٍ مَّعْلُومٍ` are the visible triggers. B003 contributes the social role of a group of drinkers/co-waterers; the abductive bridge is that named alternating turns organize distinct users around the same resource. The target thus carries obligation toward the other participant along with entitlement.

**Negative evidence.** The window supplies no concrete trigger for B004-B014, and actual imbibing under B001 is entailed by the ordinary water-share sense rather than a surprising secondary activation.

## 36:73 — وَلَهُمْ فِيهَا مَنَٰفِعُ وَمَشَارِبُ أَفَلَا يَشْكُرُونَ

### مَشَارِبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The plural denotes drinks obtained from the livestock, alongside their other benefits.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sequence moves from human ownership and control of livestock to dependence on what those animals yield, then exposes the mismatch between received benefit and the taking of powerless gods.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B012` (wealth consumed in feeding and watering) is retained. It presents the drinks not only as liquids but as the consumable yield of owned living wealth.

**Activation trace.** `أَنْعَٰمًا ... مَٰلِكُونَ` supplies livestock and property, while `فَمِنْهَا رَكُوبُهُمْ وَمِنْهَا يَأْكُلُونَ` joins use, eating, and the subsequent `مَشَارِبُ`. B012's role is economic/provisionary. Because the window explicitly frames the animals as property whose yield is eaten and drunk, the branch bridges livestock wealth to sustenance expended for its owners; the target becomes evidence of endowed provision and hence sharpens `أَفَلَا يَشْكُرُونَ`.

**Negative evidence.** Plural beneficiaries alone do not establish B003, and no drinking place, vessel, bad-water quality, permeation, fastening, or technical land/waterskin image is visible.

## 37:46 — بَيْضَآءَ لَذَّةٍ لِّلشَّٰرِبِينَ

### شَّٰرِبِينَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The participle identifies those enjoying the white, pleasurable drink passed around from a flowing source.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Pleasure is defined by subtraction as well as presence: the following ayah denies both an intrinsic affliction in the drink and depletion of the drinkers, so delight is inseparable from preserved faculties.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained contrastively. The window invokes that branch's compromised-drink profile only to reverse it: this drink is pleasant and consequence-free.

**Activation trace.** `لَذَّةٍ` directly supplies pleasant taste, while `لَا فِيهَا غَوْلٌ وَلَا هُمْ عَنْهَا يُنزَفُونَ` explicitly negates harm and depletion. B005 serves as a rejected quality-frame; the abductive bridge is that these unusually explicit assurances answer the category of liquid that can be drunk despite defect or aversion. The target therefore denotes drinkers whose drinking involves neither forced tolerance nor bodily loss.

**Negative evidence.** `كَأْسٍ` and `مَعِينٍ` belong to the primary serving scene and do not by themselves activate the broader locative B004; color alone does not establish permeation under B007. No other branch has a local trigger.

## 38:42 — ٱرْكُضْ بِرِجْلِكَ هَٰذَا مُغْتَسَلٌۢ بَارِدٌ وَشَرَابٌ

### شَرَابٌ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes cool water to drink, paired with water for washing.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Relief from Job's bodily distress is enacted through one immediately indicated water provision with outward and inward uses; restoration then expands into restored family, mercy, memory, and vindicated patience.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B004` (drinking place or source) is retained. It makes the drink part of an encountered water-site, not merely a detached serving of liquid.

**Activation trace.** The bodily command `ٱرْكُضْ بِرِجْلِكَ`, demonstrative `هَٰذَا`, and paired predicate `مُغْتَسَلٌ بَارِدٌ وَشَرَابٌ` visibly locate wash and drink in the result of the commanded strike. B004 supplies source/place as its role. The bridge is that a deictically presented provision serving both bathing and drinking behaves as a water source; the target consequently participates in a total restorative environment, internal as well as external.

**Negative evidence.** Coolness does not activate unpleasant-water B005, and bodily restoration alone is insufficient for permeation B007 or the throat-part branch B006. No other branch passes the local-trigger standard.

## 38:51 — مُتَّكِـِٔينَ فِيهَا يَدْعُونَ فِيهَا بِفَٰكِهَةٍ كَثِيرَةٍ وَشَرَابٍ

### شَرَابٍ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes drink requested and enjoyed with abundant fruit by the reclining inhabitants of the gardens.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The drink belongs to an order of unimpeded access: opened gates, repose, abundant requested fruit, companionship, and a promised outcome on the day of account.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `يَدْعُونَ ... بِفَٰكِهَةٍ كَثِيرَةٍ وَشَرَابٍ` anchors plentiful feast-service and strengthens the primary reading, but it does not visibly introduce a distinct branch of ش ر ب.

**Negative evidence.** There is no explicit property/expenditure frame for B012, no source or vessel for B004, no group-of-waterers construction for B003, and no bad-water, permeation, restraint, or technical cue for the remaining branches.

## 52:19 — كُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟ هَنِيٓـًٔۢا بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ

### ٱشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The imperative commands the garden's people to drink freely and wholesomely as recompense for what they did.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Eating and drinking are framed simultaneously as gift and exact recompense: the Lord has given and protected them, yet each person remains bound to what was earned.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B012` (wealth consumed in feeding and watering) is retained. It recasts drinking as participation in provision being expended on a feast.

**Activation trace.** The exact pair `كُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟`, preceded by `بِمَآ ءَاتَىٰهُمْ رَبُّهُمْ`, visibly combines eating, drinking, and bestowed provision; `بِمَا كُنتُمْ تَعْمَلُونَ` gives that provision a recompensive frame. B012 contributes the image of resources used to feed and water people. The bridge is that bestowed reward is now consumed through the paired acts named by that branch, so the target registers reception of an apportioned bounty, not thirst-quenching alone.

**Negative evidence.** The plural audience does not by itself activate the co-drinker branch, and the window has no source, vessel, defective water, absorption, fastening, or other specific branch trigger.

## 56:54 — فَشَٰرِبُونَ عَلَيْهِ مِنَ ٱلْحَمِيمِ

### شَٰرِبُونَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The participle states that, after filling their bellies with zaqqum, they will drink scalding water upon it.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The sequence mimics hospitality through food, drink, and `نُزُلُهُمْ`, but every stage compounds need and punishment; drinking does not relieve the meal but extends it into the thirst-pattern of `ٱلْهِيمِ`.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained in an intensified form. It makes the act necessity-drinking of a liquid whose aversive quality is foregrounded.

**Activation trace.** The object `ٱلْحَمِيمِ` (root ح م م, hot water), the prior filling with `زَقُّومٍ`, and the following simile `شُرْبَ ٱلْهِيمِ` visibly combine a hostile liquid with unrelieved thirst. B005 contributes compelled tolerance of bad water. The bridge is that the drinkers continue consuming precisely what should repel them because their thirst drives them; the target becomes punitive compulsion rather than refreshment.

**Negative evidence.** Repetition of plural drinking does not by itself establish a distinct co-drinker relation, and there is no share, place, absorption, rope, or other local trigger for the remaining ش ر ب branches.

### حَمِيمِ — ح م م

**Primary reading.** The noun denotes intensely hot or scalding water drunk as punishment.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Its placement after belly-filling and before drinking like the thirsty `هِيم` makes heat function as an anti-refreshment: the offered liquid magnifies the need it appears to answer.

**Water-root resonance.** No secondary water-root branch retained.

**Activation trace.** `فَشَٰرِبُونَ`, `شُرْبَ ٱلْهِيمِ`, and the meal sequence make the hot-water sense of حميم concrete, but these are effects and uses of B002's primary hot-water image rather than triggers for another ح م م branch.

**Negative evidence.** The window contains no blackness/charcoal, rendered fat, sweat or summer rain, fever, approaching event, animal sound, pursuit, explicit decree or death, intimate friend, bird, bathhouse, or choice-property cue. `يَوْمَ ٱلدِّينِ` alone is too broad to retain B010.

## 56:55 — فَشَٰرِبُونَ شُرْبَ ٱلْهِيمِ

### شَٰرِبُونَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The plural participle states that the punished people drink, with the following cognate phrase specifying their manner as the drinking of desperately thirsty `هِيم`.

**Ayah-level surprise.** Their repeated drinking follows full bellies and hot water yet provides no satiety; the meal's apparent abundance is exposed as a cycle of compulsive excess.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained. It casts the drinkers as consuming an aversive liquid under necessity rather than choosing a beverage.

**Activation trace.** The preceding object `ٱلْحَمِيمِ` gives the liquid's hostile heat, and `شُرْبَ ٱلْهِيمِ` gives the visible model of pathological or extreme thirst. B005 supplies necessity-drinking despite bad quality; the bridge is that the simile explains why the group drinks the scalding liquid. The target therefore marks loss of control and failed relief.

**Negative evidence.** The plural form is already the primary designation of the drinkers and does not, without reciprocal watering roles, justify B003. No other branch has a visible trigger.

### شُرْبَ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The cognate noun specifies the mode or intensity of their drinking: they drink as the thirst-driven `هِيم` drink.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The measure of punishment is not a fixed portion but an appetite that cannot complete itself, turning the usual relief of drinking into the image of endless need.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B005` (drinkable but unpleasant water) is retained. It adds the liquid-quality and necessity dimension to the target's primary manner-of-drinking sense.

**Activation trace.** `مِنَ ٱلْحَمِيمِ` in the immediately preceding ayah identifies the aversive hot liquid, while the governing `فَشَٰرِبُونَ` and comparison `ٱلْهِيمِ` make compelled, excessive intake visible. B005's role is the tolerated-but-hostile drink. The bridge is that a thirst likened to the `هِيم` accounts for consuming such water; the target comes to describe necessity overriding aversion, not quantity alone.

**Negative evidence.** The adjacent plural participles are drinking vocabulary but supply no reciprocal co-watering relation for B003; they therefore do not meet the secondary-evidence standard. No share, source, vessel, permeation, or other branch cue appears.

## 69:24 — كُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟ هَنِيٓـًٔۢا بِمَآ أَسْلَفْتُمْ فِى ٱلْأَيَّامِ ٱلْخَالِيَةِ

### ٱشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The imperative commands wholesome drinking in the high garden as reward for deeds sent ahead in former days.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The low-hanging fruit and immediate feast convert what was previously `أَسْلَفْتُمْ` into present enjoyment, while the abrupt next scene of the left-handed recipient shows the account's opposite outcome.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B012` (wealth consumed in feeding and watering) is retained. It makes drinking the realization and consumption of provision corresponding to what the recipients advanced beforehand.

**Activation trace.** The exact pair `كُلُوا۟ وَٱشْرَبُوا۟` activates B012's eating-and-drinking expenditure image, while `بِمَآ أَسْلَفْتُمْ` and the following `حِسَابِيَهْ` create a visible advance/account frame. B012 supplies the role of provision spent in feeding and watering. The abductive bridge is that former advances return as a feast now consumed; the target thus participates in a recompensive economy rather than denoting liquid intake alone.

**Negative evidence.** The garden and near fruit do not establish a drinking site under B004, and no defective-water, co-waterer, permeation, fastening, or technical cue supports another branch.

## 76:5 — إِنَّ ٱلْأَبْرَارَ يَشْرَبُونَ مِن كَأْسٍ كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا كَافُورًا

### يَشْرَبُ — ش ر ب

**Primary reading.** The verb states that the righteous drink from a cup whose mixture is camphor, in contrast to the punishment prepared for the ungrateful.

**Ayah-level surprise.** The cup is not an isolated luxury: the next ayah opens it into a spring that God's servants drink from and cause to flow, while the preceding chains and shackles sharpen the contrast between freely handled provision and imposed restraint.

**Water-root resonance.** `root_000783/B007` (permeating or being imbued), `root_000783/B004` (drinking place or source), and contrastive `root_000783/B008` (putting a rope around the neck) are retained. B007 makes the drink an imbued mixture; B004 connects the cup to an accessible, flowing source; B008 makes drinking a sign of unbound reception against the fettered alternative.

**Activation trace.** For B007, `كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا كَافُورًا` explicitly names mixture (`مِزَاج`, root م ز ج), providing the physical blending bridge by which the cup is permeated with camphor; the target becomes drinking a deliberately infused composition. For B004, the continuation `عَيْنًا يَشْرَبُ بِهَا ... يُفَجِّرُونَهَا تَفْجِيرًا` names a spring (`عَيْنًا`, root ع ي ن) and its caused flowing (`ف ج ر`); the appositional continuation bridges cup to source, so the target includes access to and agency over the drink's fountain. For B008, the immediately preceding `سَلَٰسِلَا۟ وَأَغْلَٰلًا` supplies literal chains and neck-fetters in the explicit unbeliever/righteous contrast; B008's fastening image is abductively reversed, so the target's bodily reception of the cup signals freedom from imposed binding.

**Negative evidence.** B008 is retained only because the local fetter language and contrast are unusually explicit; no animal-rope scenario is asserted as the primary sense. The window gives no water-share, unpleasant-water, neck-craning, false-charge, understanding, wealth-expenditure, waterskin, or watered-land trigger.
