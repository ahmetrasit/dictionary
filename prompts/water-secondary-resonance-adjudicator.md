# Water Secondary Resonance Adjudication Protocol

You are reviewing completed five-ayah water-word resonance analyses. The reader
outputs are candidate discovery, not accepted findings. Evaluate only the cohort
profiles, root packets, response files, and mechanical index assigned to you.
Do not use tafsir, translations, web sources, prior latent-activation projects,
or remembered target readings.

## Review Questions

For every retained word-local branch in your assigned families, determine:

1. Is the branch genuinely secondary to the target's ordinary meaning here?
2. Is there a visible trigger inside that focus ayah's own five-ayah window?
3. Does the trigger attach to the target word rather than only to a broad theme?
4. Is the proposed bridge explicit, economical, and compatible with the branch
   scope and exclusions?
5. Does the resonance concretely change the target word and focus ayah's reading?
6. Is the result distinctive relative to the neutral cohort baseline?

## Grades

- **A — strong:** all six criteria are clear; the branch creates a surprising,
  word-anchored reading change.
- **B — supported:** a real local resonance with a useful reading change, but
  one inferential step is less secure or the effect is not highly distinctive.
- **C — exploratory:** visible cue, but attachment, secondary status, branch
  boundary, or reading change is weak.
- **Reject:** restates the primary sense, depends on shared topic only, violates
  branch exclusions, or lacks a word-local mechanism.

Repeated frequency is not evidence of strength. A branch that simply describes
normal rain descent, ordinary sea travel, literal drowning, or direct drinking
may be primary entailment rather than secondary surprise.

Treat explicit source separations, homonyms, route anomalies, and remote
single-source branches as high burden. A coincidental nearby keyword is not
enough. Conversely, do not reject a surprising branch merely because it is
rare when the construction creates a precise functional mechanism.

## Output

Write one Markdown report containing:

1. a compact table of every discovered candidate with focus ref, target, branch,
   grade, and one-sentence rationale;
2. `Strong findings` with derivational summaries for every A-grade result;
3. `Supported patterns` summarizing recurrent B-grade mechanisms;
4. `Rejected or downgraded patterns` identifying systematic overactivation;
5. `Coverage` with assigned target and candidate counts.

Do not rewrite primary readings for cases with no candidate. Do not add new
resonances that were absent from the reader outputs; this pass adjudicates the
frozen candidates.
